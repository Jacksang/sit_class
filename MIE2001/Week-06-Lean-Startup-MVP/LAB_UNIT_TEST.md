## Unit Test: Week 6 Concepts (10 min)

**Name:** _________________  **Date:** _________________

### Part A: Multiple Choice (4 points)

1. The Build-Measure-Learn loop is designed to:
   - a) Build as much as possible before measuring
   - b) Minimize time between building and learning
   - c) Maximize features before launch
   - d) Ensure product quality before testing

2. Which of the following is an example of a Concierge MVP?
   - a) A pre-launch landing page to collect emails
   - b) Manually delivering a service to understand customer needs
   - c) A fully functional app with basic features
   - d) A paper sketch of the user interface

3. The "riskiest assumption" is:
   - a) Any assumption that might be wrong
   - b) The assumption that, if wrong, would invalidate your business
   - c) The assumption about your target market size
   - d) The technical assumption about your product

4. Validated learning means:
   - a) Learning from reading books and articles
   - b) Evidence-based progress through experiments
   - c) Confirming your initial hypothesis was correct
   - d) Getting feedback from friends and family

### Part B: Short Answer (4 points)

5. Explain why "Our MVP needs a login system" is a common MVP mistake. What should the MVP focus on instead? (2 points)

6. Give one example of an MVP that you could build WITHOUT writing any code. What assumption would it test? (2 points)

### Part C: Application (2 points)

7. You have an idea: a platform that connects SIT students with alumni mentors.

Identify the riskiest assumption and design an MVP to test it.

**Riskiest assumption:**

___________________________________________________________

**MVP design (what will you do, how will you measure):**

___________________________________________________________

### Answer Key
1. b
2. b
3. b
4. b
5. Login systems add friction and don't test your core assumption; MVP should test whether students want lunch recommendations, not whether they can log in
6. Open-ended — any valid example (landing page, Google Form, personal outreach, etc.)
7. Open-ended — check that MVP is genuinely minimal (e.g., manual matching of 5 students with alumni via email, not building a platform)
