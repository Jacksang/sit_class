# Week 6 — Extension Reading & Enrichment

## Core Reading (Required)

**"The Lean Startup"** — Eric Ries (Ch 6-8)
- Build-Measure-Learn loop in depth
- The chapter on "Leap" (assumption testing) is especially relevant

## Enrichment Readings

### Lean Startup Theory
1. **"The Lean Startup" (Full Book)** — Eric Ries
   - Ch 3: "Learn" — validated learning explained
   - Ch 8: "Measure" — actionable metrics vs. vanity metrics
   - Ch 9: "Pivot (or Persevere)" — decision framework

2. **"Running Lean"** — Ash Maurya (Ch 1-5)
   - Practical workbook version of Lean Startup
   - Lean Canvas template, experiment design
   - More actionable than Ries for early-stage founders

3. **"The Startup Way"** — Eric Ries
   - Applying lean startup inside established organizations
   - Good context for students who might work in corporate innovation

### MVP Design
4. **"Sprint: How to Solve Big Problems and Test New Ideas in Just 5 Days"** — Jake Knapp
   - Google Ventures' sprint methodology
   - Day 1-3: problem definition, Day 4: prototype, Day 5: test with 5 users

5. **"MVP Guide for Non-Technical Founders"** — Y Combinator blog
   - Practical examples of non-technical MVPs
   - No-code tools, spreadsheets, manual services

6. **"The Concierge MVP: How to Validate Your Startup Without Writing Code"** — Andrew Chen
   - Why manual service MVPs often work better than automated ones
   - Case studies from early-stage startups

### Singapore MVP Stories
7. **"How We Built the MVP for [Singapore Startup] in One Weekend"** — Medium (search for your specific startup)
   - HonestBell, Homage, Doctor Anywhere MVPs
   - Each shows how they started small

8. **"The No-Code MVP Revolution in Southeast Asia"** — e27
   - How SEA founders use no-code tools (Carrd, Bubble, Telegram bots)
   - Lowering the barrier to building MVPs
