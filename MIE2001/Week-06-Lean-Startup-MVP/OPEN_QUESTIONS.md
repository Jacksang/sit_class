## Open Questions for Discussion

### Conceptual Questions
1. **Build-Measure-Learn speed:** How fast should a student team go through one loop? Is one loop per week realistic, or too fast?

2. **MVP vs. Polished:** When is a "rough" MVP a risk (people don't take you seriously) vs. an advantage (you get honest feedback because it looks scrappy)?

3. **Pivot vs. Persevere:** How many failed MVPs before you pivot? Is there a rule of thumb, or do you just "feel it"?

### Application Questions
4. **Your riskiest assumption:** After identifying it, does the idea of testing it make you excited or anxious? What does your reaction tell you?

5. **The "wizard behind the curtain":** If you use a Wizard of Oz MVP (users think it's automated, but you're doing it manually), is that deceptive? Or is it fair as long as you eventually deliver?

6. **MVP failure coping:** If your MVP fails (no sign-ups, no interest), how do you separate "the idea is wrong" from "the test was wrong"?

### Singapore Context Questions
7. In Singapore, many students are not used to "shipping imperfect work" — grades reward refinement. How does this mindset conflict with lean startup's "ship early, ship ugly" philosophy?

8. WhatsApp and Telegram are dominant in Singapore. How does this affect MVP strategy? Should every Singapore student startup consider a Telegram bot as their first MVP?
