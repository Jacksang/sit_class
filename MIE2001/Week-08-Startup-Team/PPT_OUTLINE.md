## Part 2: Why Teams Matter (15 min)

### Slide 4 — Investors Bet on Teams, Not Ideas
- **Data point:** 65% of startup failures are due to team problems (founder conflict, bad hiring, lack of co-founder)
- **Data point:** Startups with 2+ founders raise 30% more money on average
- **VC saying:** "I'd rather invest in an A-team with a B-idea than a B-team with an A-idea"

### Slide 5 — What Makes a Great Startup Team?
| Quality | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| Complementary skills | Not two people who do the same thing |
| Shared values | You agree on what matters (ethics, pace, communication) |
| Conflict resolution | You can disagree without destroying the relationship |
| Mutual respect | You trust each other's judgment |
| Complementary networks | You know different people in different circles |

## Part 3: Equity & Co-Founder Dynamics (25 min)

### Slide 7 — Equity Splits: Fair is Not Always Equal
- **Myth:** "We should split 50/50 because we're friends"
- **Reality:** Equity should reflect contribution, commitment, and risk
- **Factors to consider:**
  - Who had the idea? (worth less than you think — execution > idea)
  - Who's working full-time vs. part-time?
  - Who's bringing capital?
  - Who has the relevant network or expertise?
  - Who will do the unglamorous work?

### Slide 8 — Common Equity Split Models
| Model | Description | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **50/50** | Equal split | Two co-founders with equal commitment and contribution |
| **60/40** | Majority/minority | One leader, one supporter — but clear roles |
| **Dynamic equity** | Based on actual contributions over time | Uncertain commitment levels early on |
| **Vesting** | Earn equity over time (4 years typical) | Preventing early departures |

**Key concept:** Vesting means you earn your shares. If you leave in Year 1, you keep 25% of your shares. If you stay 4 years, you keep 100%.

### Slide 9 — Dynamic Equity Exercise
- **Scenario:** You and two friends want to start a business. Roles:
  - Person A: Full-time, experienced, bringing \$20K
  - Person B: Part-time (20 hrs/week), technical
  - Person C: Part-time (10 hrs/week), marketing, great connections
- **Activity (5 min):** How would you split equity? Discuss in teams, then share

**No "right answer"** — but discuss principles: fair, motivating, and sustainable.

### Slide 10 — Vesting & Cliff Explained
- **Cliff:** You don't get any equity until you've stayed 1 year (standard)
  - If you quit before 1 year — 0 equity
  - If you stay 1 year — you get 1 year's worth (vested)
- **Schedule:** Monthly vesting after cliff (so equity vests gradually)
- **Why it matters:** Prevents someone from leaving early with a large chunk of equity

**Student context:** For a semester-long project, think about "effort vesting" — what if someone stops contributing in Week 10? What happens?

## Part 4: Team Charter (30 min)

### Slide 11 — What is a Team Charter?
- **Definition:** A written agreement that defines how your team works together
- **NOT a legal contract** — it's a commitment to shared expectations
- **Why you need one:** Most team conflicts come from unspoken expectations

### Slide 12 — Team Charter Template
**Our Team Charter**

**1. Team Members & Roles**
| Name | Primary Role | Secondary Role |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| | Research & Customer Discovery | Presentation |
| | Technical / MVP Building | Design |
| | Business Model & Finance | Writing |
| | Project Management | Pitching |

**2. Meeting & Communication**
- How often will we meet outside class? _______________
- How will we communicate between meetings? (Telegram? WhatsApp? Notion?) _______________
- Decision-making method: (consensus? majority vote? founder decides?) _______________

**3. Work Standards**
- What does "done" look like for our deliverables? _______________
- How will we handle missed deadlines? _______________
- What happens if someone doesn't pull their weight? _______________

**4. Conflict Resolution**
- If two of us disagree: _______________
- If the whole team disagrees: _______________
- How do we give each other feedback? _______________

**5. Values**
- What's most important to us as a team? (circle 3: speed, quality, fun, learning, fairness, ambition, support)

### Slide 13 — Charter Negotiation Activity (25 min)

- **Hard conversations (10 min):** Discuss the "what happens if someone doesn't pull their weight" section — this is the most important one
- **Sign it (5 min):** Print and sign. This becomes your commitment for the rest of the semester.

## Part 5: Ethics in Entrepreneurship (20 min)

### Slide 14 — Why Ethics Matter
- Startups operate in a "gray zone" — fast, unregulated, ambiguous
- **Singapore context:** Strong regulatory framework, but enforcement is reactive
- **Key question:** Will you make decisions you're proud of?

### Slide 15 — Common Ethical Dilemmas for Student Startups
| Dilemma | Question to Ask Yourself |
|---------|------------------------|
| "Should we fake it till we make it?" | What's the line between marketing and deception? |
| "Our competitor's data would be useful..." | Would I be comfortable if this was public? |
| "This user said yes to our interview but we didn't ask permission to record" | Consent is ongoing, not one-time |
| "My friend gave me their idea — can I run with it?" | Did they explicitly share it, or was it confidential? |
| "We launched but the product doesn't work yet" | Have you been clear about limitations? |

### Slide 16 — Ethical Decision-Making Framework
1. **Identify the stakeholders** — who is affected?
2. **Check the law** — is it legal in Singapore?
3. **Check your values** — would you be OK if this was on the news?
4. **Test with a trusted person** — ask someone not in your team
5. **Make the decision** — and explain it transparently

### Slide 17 — Singapore Regulatory Context
- **ACRA:** Company registration, financial reporting
- **IPOS:** Intellectual Property Office — patents, trademarks
- **PDPC:** Personal Data Protection — data collection and use
- **MOM:** Employment regulations (if hiring)

**For student projects:** You likely don't need to register a company yet. But understand these for your eventual launch.
