# Week 8 — Lab & Unit Test Design

## Lab: Team Charter Negotiation (40 min)

### Objective
Create a signed team charter that defines roles, communication, work standards, and conflict resolution.

### Part A: Pre-Lab Prep (done before class — 10 min)
Each team member completes this "Team Values" worksheet individually:

**1. My preferred role (rank 1-3):**
- [ ] Customer discovery & research
- [ ] MVP building & technical work
- [ ] Business model & finance
- [ ] Writing & documentation
- [ ] Presenting & pitching
- [ ] Project management & deadlines

**2. Communication preferences:**
- How quickly should we respond to messages? (within 2h? 12h? 24h?)
- Best time for team meetings: _______________
- Preferred communication tool: _______________

**3. Work style:**
- I prefer to work: (alone first, then collaborate / together the whole time / async only)
- I get stressed when: _______________
- I get motivated when: _______________

**4. Red lines (non-negotiable):**
___________________________________________________________

### Part B: Charter Creation (20 min, team)
Complete the Team Charter template (see PPT outline Slide 12). Discuss each section:

**Must-cover topics:**
1. Roles (who does what?)
2. Meeting schedule & communication
3. Decision-making process
4. Missed deadline procedure
5. Conflict resolution process
6. Values (what matters most)

**Facilitation tips:**
- If you're stuck on a topic, move to the next one and come back
- If someone disagrees, ask: "What would make this acceptable to you?"
- Make sure everyone speaks at least once

### Part C: Hard Conversation (10 min, team)
Discuss the "what if someone doesn't pull their weight" section specifically.

**Scenario exercise:**
- "It's Week 11. One team member has missed two deadlines. They say they're busy with other courses. What do we do?"

**Discuss:**
1. First step: _______________
2. Second step: _______________
3. Escalation: _______________

### Part D: Signature & Reminder (10 min)
1. Write final version of charter (5 min)
2. All team members sign (2 min)
3. Submit photo/scan (3 min)

**Post-charter:** Take a photo with your charter — post it to your team chat as a reminder.

### Lab Deliverable
Submit: Signed Team Charter (PDF/photo)

## Unit Test: Week 8 Concepts (10 min)

**Name:** _________________  **Date:** _________________

### Part A: Multiple Choice (4 points)

1. Research on startup success shows that:
   - a) Having a single founder is more successful
   - b) Team quality is more important than the initial idea
   - c) Equity should always be split equally
   - d) Co-founders should have the same skills

2. A "vesting schedule" means:
   - a) You get all your equity on Day 1
   - b) You earn your equity over time
   - c) You give equity to investors
   - d) You split equity based on seniority

3. The main purpose of a team charter is to:
   - a) Create a legally binding contract
   - b) Set shared expectations for how the team works
   - c) Assign grades to team members
   - d) Register the company

4. A "cliff" in startup equity means:
   - a) You can leave anytime without penalty
   - b) No equity vests until you've stayed a minimum period (usually 1 year)
   - c) Equity is distributed at the end of the company
   - d) The cliff only applies to investors

### Part B: Short Answer (4 points)

5. Why do investors say they prefer A-teams with B-ideas over B-teams with A-ideas? (2 points)

6. Name one factor that should be considered when splitting equity (beyond "it should be equal"). (2 points)

### Part C: Application (2 points)

7. You and your co-founder disagree about whether to build a full-featured app (6 months) or a simple MVP (2 weeks). Your co-founder wants the full app. Using the Team Charter framework, explain how you would resolve this disagreement.
