*A student-friendly guide to local resources, contacts, and opportunities*

## 1. Government Agencies & Support

### Enterprise Singapore (EnterpriseSG)
- **What they do:** Help startups grow locally and internationally
- **Key programs:**
  - Startup SG Founder — $50k matching grant for first-time founders
  - Startup SG Tech — grant for tech commercialization
  - Market Readiness Assistance (MRA) — grants for going overseas
- **Website:** enterprisesg.gov.sg
- **Relevance:** Any student starting a venture should know about these

### ACE.SG (Action Community for Entrepreneurship)
- **What they do:** Coordinate the startup ecosystem
- **Key programs:**
  - Startup SG Network — events, mentorship, connections
  - ACE.SG mentorship program
  - Global Startup Directory
- **Website:** ace.gov.sg
- **Relevance:** Best starting point for student founders

### SGInnovate
- **What they do:** Support deep tech startups (AI, biotech, etc.)
- **Key programs:**
  - 1000 Eyes — deep tech matching
  - Talent programs, workshops
  - Investor matching
- **Website:** sginnovate.com

## 7. Legal & IP in Singapore

### Company Registration
- **ACRA (Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority)** — Register your company
- **Cost:** $115 (one-time)
- **Time:** 15 minutes to 1 day
- **Student discount:** None, but easy to DIY

### Intellectual Property
- **IPOS (Intellectual Property Office of Singapore)** — Patents, trademarks, designs
- **Student rates:** Patent filing fees reduced for students
- **SIT I&E office:** Free IP consultation for SIT students

### Employment Pass (for hiring foreigners)
- **Minimum salary:** $5,000/month
- **Reality for students:** Don't hire foreigners early; focus on Singaporeans first
- **Alternative:** Engage interns (no pass needed for short-term)

## 8. Quick Tips for Student Entrepreneurs

**💰 Starting costs in Singapore:**
- Registration: $115
- Basic bank account: $0 (DBS, OCBC)
- Simple website: $0 (Carrd) to $12/year (domain)
- MVP landing page: $0 (Netlify, Vercel)
- Coworking: $0 (SIT campus) to $80/mo (Space@Tampines)
- **Total to start:** Less than $200

**🏆 First customer strategies:**
1. Friends and family (but don't be pushy)
2. Carousell — test demand with a listing
3. Telegram groups — join relevant communities
4. LinkedIn — DM your ideal customer profile
5. Campus — sell to fellow students first

**🚫 Common mistakes first-time founders make:**
1. Building before validating
2. Raising money before proving demand
3. Hiring too fast
4. Ignoring legal/contract basics
5. Taking no salary vs. taking too much salary
6. Not talking to customers every week
