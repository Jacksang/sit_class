## Part 2: BMC Deep Dive — All 9 Blocks (30 min)

### Slide 4 — Block 1: Customer Segments
**Question:** Who are we creating value for?

- **Key insight:** Know your segment specifically (from Week 9)
- **Examples:**
  - Carousell: Young Southeast Asians decluttering and bargain-hunting
  - Grab: Urban commuters who need reliable transport
  - Your venture: [specific customer segment]

**Common mistake:** "Everyone" — you don't serve everyone. Be specific.

### Slide 5 — Block 2: Value Propositions
**Question:** What value do we deliver to the customer?

- **Types of value:** Newness, performance, customization, "getting the job done", design, brand/status, price, cost reduction, risk reduction, accessibility, convenience/usability
- **Exercise:** "In one sentence, why should a customer choose YOU over alternatives?"

### Slide 6 — Block 3: Channels
**Question:** How do we reach our customer segments?

- **From Week 9:** Your top 3 channels
- **Put them here:** Awareness, evaluation, purchase, delivery, after-sales

### Slide 7 — Block 4: Customer Relationships
**Question:** What relationship does each customer segment expect?

- **Types:** Personal assistance, dedicated personal assistance, self-service, automated services, communities, co-creation
- **Student example:** WhatsApp group (community) + personal onboarding
- **Key question:** "Is our relationship model scalable?"

### Slide 8 — Block 5: Revenue Streams
**Question:** How does each customer segment pay?

- **From Week 10:** Your revenue model(s)
- **Types:** Asset sale, usage fee, subscription, lending/leasing, licensing, brokerage, advertising
- **Key check:** Does your revenue model match your customer's willingness to pay?

### Slide 9 — Block 6: Key Resources
**Question:** What unique resources do we need to deliver our value?

- **Physical:** Equipment, inventory
- **Intellectual:** Brand, patents, data, proprietary knowledge
- **Human:** Skills, expertise, team
- **Financial:** Cash, credit, funding

**Student reality:** Your key resource is your TIME and your TEAM. You have more than you think.

### Slide 10 — Block 7: Key Activities
**Question:** What are the most important things we need to DO?

- **Production:** Making the product
- **Problem-solving:** Custom solutions
- **Platform/network:** Maintaining a marketplace or platform

**Exercise:** "What do we spend most of our time on?" — that should be your key activity.

### Slide 11 — Block 8: Key Partnerships
**Question:** Who can help us deliver our value?

- **From Week 2 (Crazy Quilt):** Partnerships are how startups survive
- **Types:**
  - Strategic alliances (non-competitors)
  - Coopetition (partnership with competitors)
  - Joint ventures (new entity)
  - Buyer-supplier relationships
- **Singapore-specific partners:** SIT I&E Office, industry partners, alumni network

### Slide 12 — Block 9: Cost Structure
**Question:** What are the most important costs in our business?

- **From Week 10:** Your financial model
- **Cost-driven vs. value-driven:** Are you minimizing costs or maximizing value?
- **Fixed vs. variable:** Fixed (rent, salaries) vs. variable (raw materials, marketing spend)

**Key check:** Does your cost structure align with your value proposition?

### Slide 13 — Coherence Check
**The most important question:** Do all 9 blocks tell ONE consistent story?

**Example — A luxury hotel:**
- Customer: wealthy travelers ✓
- Value: premium experience ✓
- Cost: high staffing, premium location ✓
- Revenue: high room rates ✓
- → Coherent story

**Example — Same hotel with budget pricing:**
- Customer: wealthy travelers
- Value: luxury
- Cost: high
- Revenue: budget pricing ← **NOT coherent!**

**Your turn:** Read your BMC left-to-right. Does it make sense as a story?

## Part 3: Value Proposition Canvas (15 min)

### Slide 14 — Zooming Into the Value Proposition
- The VPC is a tool that sits "inside" your BMC
- It focuses on the relationship between Customer Segment and Value Proposition

### Slide 15 — VPC: Customer Profile
| Customer Jobs | Pains | Gains |
|--------------|-------|--------|
| What is the customer trying to get done? | What frustrates them? | What do they WANT to achieve? |
| Functional, social, emotional | Risks, obstacles, negative emotions | Desired outcomes, benefits |

### Slide 16 — VPC: Value Map
| Products & Services | Pain Relievers | Gain Creators |
|--------------------|----------------|---------------|
| What do we offer? | How do we fix their pains? | How do we create their desired gains? |

### Slide 17 — VPC Fit
**Fit occurs when:** Your Pain Relievers match their Pains, AND your Gain Creators match their Gains.

**Check:** Look at your customer interview notes. Do you have evidence for each pain and gain you've listed?

## Part 4: BMC Gallery Walk (40 min)

### Slide 18 — Gallery Walk Instructions
- **Format:** Half the teams present, half visit → then switch
- **Time:** 5 min per team presenting, 5 min per team visiting
- **Presenting team:** Pin your BMC on the wall (or display on screen)
  - 2-min walkthrough: "Here's our model, here's where we need feedback"
  - 3-min Q&A
- **Visiting teams:** Walk around, look at others' BMCs
  - Leave 1-2 sticky notes on each BMC:
    - **Green sticky:** Something that works well
    - **Yellow sticky:** A question you have
    - **Red sticky:** Something that seems inconsistent

### Slide 19 — Feedback Guidelines
- Be specific: "Your customer segment is too broad — who specifically?"
- Be constructive: "Have you considered adding [partnership]?"
- Be generous: "This revenue model is really clever — have you thought about [extension]?"

### Slide 20 — Rotation Schedule
*Adjust based on team count*

| Round | Presenting Teams | Visiting Teams |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1 (20 min) | Teams A-D | Teams E-H |
| 2 (20 min) | Teams E-H | Teams A-D |

### Slide 21 — After the Walk: Iteration Time (20 min)
- Review the sticky notes on your BMC
- Discuss with your team: What feedback will you incorporate?
- What feedback do you disagree with? Why?
- Make changes to your BMC now
