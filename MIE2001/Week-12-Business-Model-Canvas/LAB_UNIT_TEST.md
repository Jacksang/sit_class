# Week 12 — Lab & Unit Test Design

## Lab: BMC Gallery Walk & Iteration (45 min)

### Objective
Get peer feedback on your BMC, give feedback to others, and iterate before finals.

### Part A: BMC Finalization (15 min, teams — before gallery walk)
1. Write/draw your final BMC on a large piece of paper (or display on screen)
2. Identify 1-2 specific areas where you want feedback
3. Write "Help needed: [question]" at the top

### Part B: Gallery Walk (20 min)
**See PPT outline (Slide 18-21) for full format**

- Round 1 (10 min): Presenting teams share; visiting teams leave sticky notes
- Round 2 (10 min): Switch roles

### Part C: Iteration (10 min, teams)
1. Review the feedback sticky notes
2. Discuss: what will you incorporate? What will you set aside?
3. Make at least 1 change to your BMC based on feedback

### Lab Deliverable
Submit: Final BMC (digital or photo of physical board)

**Name:** _________________  **Date:** _________________

### Part A: Multiple Choice (4 points)

1. The Business Model Canvas consists of how many building blocks?
   - a) 7
   - b) 8
   - c) 9
   - d) 10

2. The "coherence check" for a BMC means:
   - a) All 9 blocks are filled in
   - b) The blocks tell one consistent story about the business
   - c) The blocks are color-coded
   - d) The BMC is formatted correctly

3. The Value Proposition Canvas focuses on:
   - a) All 9 BMC blocks
   - b) The relationship between Customer Segments and Value Proposition
   - c) Only the financial blocks
   - d) The competitive landscape

4. "Pain Relievers" in the VPC correspond to:
   - a) Customer Jobs
   - b) Customer Pains
   - c) Customer Gains
   - d) Products & Services

### Part B: Short Answer (4 points)

5. Name any four of the nine BMC blocks. (4 points)

### Part C: Application (2 points)

6. Your team's BMC has Customer Segments = "University students" and Channels = "LinkedIn ads, trade show booths, and cold calls." What coherence issue do you see, and how would you fix it?

---

### Answer Key
1. c
2. b
3. b
4. b
5. Any four: Customer Segments, Value Propositions, Channels, Customer Relationships, Revenue Streams, Key Resources, Key Activities, Key Partnerships, Cost Structure
6. LinkedIn ads and trade shows don't effectively reach university students; channels should match the segment (e.g., Instagram, TikTok, campus events, Telegram)
