## Open Questions for Discussion

### Conceptual Questions
1. **Causation vs. Effectuation:** Can you think of a situation where planning (causation) is *better* than effectuation? When would you use each approach?

2. **Bird-in-Hand in Singapore:** What resources do SIT students have that a student in another country might not? (e.g., government grants, SG innovative culture, proximity to ASEAN markets)

3. **Growth mindset block:** What's one belief about yourself that might be a fixed mindset trap in disguise? (e.g., "I'm not creative," "I'm bad with money," "I don't know any business people")

### Application Questions
4. **Your own identity:** If you had to start a business TODAY using only who you are, what you know, and who you know — what would it be?

5. **The surprise rule:** Think of an unexpected event from the past month. Could there be an opportunity hidden in it?

6. **Affordable loss check:** What's the most you'd invest (money, time, social capital) to test a business idea right now as a student?

### Singapore Context Questions
7. Singapore's government actively encourages entrepreneurship (Startup SG, ACE.SG grants, tax incentives). Does this make Singaporeans more entrepreneurial, or does the safety net reduce the "necessity" that drives entrepreneurship?

8. Many Singapore students prefer stable careers (civil service, MNCs) over startups. Is this a mindset issue or a rational choice given Singapore's cost of living?
