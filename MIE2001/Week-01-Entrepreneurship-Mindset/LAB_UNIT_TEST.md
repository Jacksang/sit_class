# Week 1 — Lab & Unit Test Design

## Lab: "Who Are You as an Entrepreneur?" (45 min)

### Objective
Complete your personal entrepreneurial profile. By the end, you'll have a clearer sense of your starting point for the semester.

### Part A: Self-Assessment (10 min, individual)
Fill out this quick diagnostic:

| Dimension | Score (1-5) | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| I can identify problems worth solving | 1 2 3 4 5 | |
| I'm comfortable with uncertainty | 1 2 3 4 5 | |
| I know how to talk to strangers | 1 2 3 4 5 | |
| I can convince people to try something new | 1 2 3 4 5 | |
| I can bounce back from rejection | 1 2 3 4 5 | |
| I can work with people who disagree with me | 1 2 3 4 5 | |

**Reflection:** Pick one dimension you scored lowest on. Write 2-3 sentences about how you could improve it this semester.

### Part B: Resource Inventory (15 min, individual then pairs)
**Bird-in-Hand exercise: "My Starting Point"**

**Identity (Who are you?)**
- Your degree program and what it taught you
- Your skills (technical, creative, social, organizational)
- Your personal strengths and weaknesses

**Knowledge (What do you know?)**
- Industries you've observed
- Hobbies or interests with depth
- Problems you've personally experienced

**Network (Who do you know?)**
- Family members in specific industries
- Friends with unique skills or access
- Professors, mentors, or alumni contacts

**Share:** Find a partner. Tell them your top resource in each category. Ask them: "What's one thing I'm not seeing?"

### Part C: Bad Idea → Good Idea Reframe (15 min, pairs)
Take the "bad idea" from the icebreaker. Apply these shifts:

**Reframing questions:**
1. Who might actually want this? (Any niche?)
2. What problem does it accidentally solve?
3. Could it be the starting point for something else?
4. What's the smallest version that could work?

**Example:** "Scented bricks" → Construction company that uses aromatherapy to improve worker productivity on site? → Industrial wellness consulting?

### Part D: Team Formation Preview (5 min)
Stand up. Find 2-3 people you haven't talked to yet.
- Ask: "What was your most interesting answer to Part B?"
- Exchange Telegram numbers if you might want to team up
