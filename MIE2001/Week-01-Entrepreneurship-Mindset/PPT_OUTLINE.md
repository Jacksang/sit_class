## Part 2: Defining Entrepreneurship (25 min)

### Slide 4 — What Is Entrepreneurship?
- Common answers: "Starting a company", "Being your own boss", "Risk-taking"
- **Academic definition:** The process of identifying, creating, and pursuing opportunities — regardless of resources you currently control (Stevenson)
- **Key insight:** Entrepreneurship is a *method*, not a job title

### Slide 5 — Myths vs. Reality (Interactive)
| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| Entrepreneurs are born, not made | Entrepreneurship can be learned |
| Entrepreneurs are lone geniuses | Most successful ventures are team efforts |
| Entrepreneurship = huge risk | Expert entrepreneurs *reduce* risk systematically |
| You need a billion-dollar idea | Most successful businesses solve small problems well |
| Entrepreneurship is about money | Purpose and impact drive long-term success |

**Ask class:** Which myth surprised you most? Which one did you believe?

### Slide 6 — Schumpeter's Creative Destruction
- Joseph Schumpeter (1934): Entrepreneurs as "creative destroyers"
- Old industries → disrupted → replaced by new ones
- **Examples:** Taxis → Grab, Hotels → Airbnb, Retail → Shopee
- **Discussion:** What industry is being disrupted right now in Singapore?

### Slide 7 — Modern Definition: Problem-Solving
- Entrepreneurship = identifying a problem and building a solution people will pay for
- It can happen inside a company (intrapreneurship)
- It can be non-profit (social entrepreneurship)
- It can be part-time (side hustle entrepreneurship)
- **Core idea:** You don't need to quit your job to be an entrepreneur

## Part 3: Growth Mindset (15 min)

### Slide 8 — Fixed vs. Growth Mindset (Carol Dweck)
- **Fixed mindset:** "I'm not good at this" / "Entrepreneurs are born"
- **Growth mindset:** "I can learn this" / "Failure is feedback"
- **Ask:** Which mindset describes your relationship with entrepreneurship?

### Slide 9 — Growth Mindset in Entrepreneurship
- Every successful founder has a folder of failures
- Examples:
  - Jack Ma: rejected from 30+ jobs, KFC rejected him
  - Grab founders: pivoted from taxi app to super-app
  - Singapore founders: SHOPBACK (affiliate → rewards platform)
- **Key reframe:** "I failed" → "I learned what doesn't work"

### Slide 10 — Mini Self-Assessment (2 min)
- On a scale of 1-10: "How entrepreneurial do you feel right now?"
- Write down your number. Keep it. Check again in Week 13.

## Part 4: Introduction to Effectuation (15 min)

### Slide 11 — Two Ways to Think: Causation vs. Effectuation
- **Causation:** "I have a goal. I make a plan. I execute."
  - Cook a meal: find a recipe, buy ingredients, follow steps
- **Effectuation:** "I have ingredients. What can I make?"
  - Open the fridge: what's available? What can I create?

### Slide 12 — The 5 Effectuation Principles (Preview)
| Principle | Key Idea |
|-----------|----------|
| Bird-in-Hand | Start with who you are, what you know, who you know |
| Affordable Loss | Invest only what you can afford to lose |
| Crazy Quilt | Build partnerships before you compete |
| Lemonade | Turn surprises into opportunities |
| Pilot-in-the-Plane | Focus on what you can control |

**Tell class:** We'll spend all of Week 2 on these — don't worry about memorizing today.

### Slide 13 — Why Effectuation Matters for Students
- You don't need money → you need *resourcefulness*
- You don't need a perfect plan → you need *action*
- You don't need a co-founder yet → you need *curiosity*
