# MIE2001 — Assessment Rubrics

---

## 1. Weekly Reflections (15%)

**Format:** 250 words, submitted before each week's class  
**Goal:** Encourage weekly processing, not just end-of-semester cramming

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Satisfactory (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Reflection depth** | Connects weekly activity to personal entrepreneurial journey; shows genuine introspection | Links activity to course concepts | Summarizes activity without insight | Superficial or missing |
| **Application** | Identifies how concept applies to their own idea or real-world observation | Gives one example of application | Mentions concept but no direct application | No attempt to apply |
| **Breadth of thinking** | Considers multiple perspectives, challenges own assumptions | Shows awareness of alternative views | Single perspective only | No analysis |

**Submission:** Via Notion or LMS, due Friday before next class

---

## 2. Customer Discovery Report (20%)

**Format:** 3-4 pages (group submission)  
**Goal:** Demonstrate rigorous problem validation through customer interviews

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Satisfactory (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Interview quality** | 5+ interviews with target users; open-ended questions; rich notes | 3-4 interviews, reasonably good questions | 1-2 interviews, leading questions | No interviews or irrelevant |
| **Problem validation** | Clear evidence problem exists (frequency, intensity, willingness to pay) | Some evidence, but gaps remain | Assumptions not validated | No validation attempt |
| **Learning articulation** | Shows how interviews changed their understanding of the problem | Describes what they learned | Lists interview quotes without synthesis | No learning evident |
| **Structure & clarity** | Well-organized, compelling narrative | Clear structure | Some organization | Disorganized |

**Tips for students:**
- Use "The Mom Test" techniques
- Record conversations (with permission) for reference
- A "no" from a good interview is more valuable than a "yes" from a friend

## 3. Business Model Canvas (20%)

**Format:** One-page BMC + 2-page justification (group submission)  
**Goal:** Demonstrate coherent business model thinking

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Satisfactory (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Completeness** | All 9 blocks filled with specific, relevant content | 8-9 blocks filled, minor gaps | 5-7 blocks filled | <5 blocks filled |
| **Coherence** | Blocks tell a consistent story (e.g., channels match customer segments) | Most blocks align | Some contradictions | Blocks don't fit together |
| **Evidence-based** | Each block justified by customer research | Most blocks have some justification | Some claims unsupported | No research basis |
| **Iteration** | Shows how BMC evolved from Week 5 draft | Some changes made | Minor changes | No iteration from mid-term |

**Submission:** Digital BMC (Miro/Mural export) + PDF justification

---

## 4. Pitch Deck + Presentation (25%)

**Format:** 10 slides max, 7-min presentation + 5-min Q&A (group)  
**Goal:** Convince a mock investor panel

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Satisfactory (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Storytelling** | Compelling narrative hook; clear problem-solution arc | Clear story, some emotional pull | Present but weak | No narrative structure |
| **Content** | All key elements present: problem, solution, market, traction, team, ask | One element missing | Multiple gaps | Critical elements absent |
| **Visual design** | Professional, clean, consistent branding | Good design | Basic | Distracting or unclear |
| **Delivery** | Confident, engaging, natural Q&A handling | Good delivery | Nervous but passable | Reads slides, poor Q&A |
| **Q&A** | Answers clearly, acknowledges unknowns confidently | Answers most questions well | Struggles with tough questions | Defensive or unprepared |

**Judging panel:** Instructors + guest entrepreneurs/investors (if available)

---

## 5. Participation & Peer Feedback (20%)

**Format:** Ongoing assessment + peer evaluation form

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Satisfactory (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Class participation** | Contributes meaningfully every session | Most sessions | Occasionally | Rarely or disruptively |
| **Peer feedback quality** | Specific, constructive, actionable feedback | Good feedback | Vague feedback | Negative or absent |
| **Team contribution** (peer eval) | "Would work with them again" | "Good team member" | "Did their share" | "Didn't contribute" |

**Peer evaluation form** (submitted Week 13, confidential):
- Rate each team member: 1-4 on contribution, reliability, collaboration
- Two sentence qualitative comment on each member
- Self-assessment

---

## Grading Philosophy

**70% Process, 30% Outcome**

We grade the *journey* of entrepreneurship, not just the final business idea. A failed idea with deep learning is worth more than a successful idea with no learning.

**What "A" work looks like:**
- Conducted 10+ customer interviews (even if they proved the idea wrong)
- Iterated their BMC based on real feedback
- Built and tested an MVP (even if it showed nobody wanted it)
- Presented with clarity, confidence, and self-awareness
- Contributed to their team and the class community

**What "C" work looks like:**
- Did the minimum interviews (2-3)
- BMC was completed but not iterated
- MVP was theoretical (not built/tested)
- Pitch was adequate but uninspired
- Participated inconsistently
