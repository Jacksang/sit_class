## Open Questions for Discussion

### Conceptual Questions
1. **Segmentation depth:** At what point does segmenting become *over-segmenting*? (e.g., "left-handed SIT students who eat lunch between 12:30-1:00 PM and take the MRT" — too narrow?)

2. **Persona vs. Real Person:** If your persona is based on a real person you interviewed, is it still a "persona" or just a profile? When does a persona stop being useful?

3. **Channel quantity vs. quality:** Is it better to be great at one channel (e.g., TikTok expert) or competent at three (e.g., Telegram + email + events)?

### Application Questions
4. **Your channel selection:** After doing the scoring exercise, which channel scored highest? Were you surprised?

5. **Carousell experiment:** If you were to create a Carousell listing for your MVP today, what would it look like? What would you learn from it?

6. **Channel failure:** What if your top 3 channels all fail after 2 weeks of testing? How many channels do you test before rethinking your customer segment?

### Singapore Context Questions
7. Telegram is dominant in Singapore but not in most Western markets. Does relying on Telegram mean your marketing playbook wouldn't work outside SG?

8. In Singapore's high-density urban environment, many "offline" channels (campus booths, posters, events) are very cheap. Are students under-using these in favor of online channels?
