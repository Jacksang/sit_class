## Part 2: Customer Segmentation (20 min)

### Slide 4 — Why Customer Segmentation Matters
- **You cannot sell to everyone** — even Coca-Cola has segments
- **Segmentation = focus, focus = efficiency**
- **Segmentation leads to personalization** — better messaging, better product
- **Segmentation reduces waste** — don't market to people who won't buy

### Slide 5 — Types of Segmentation
| Type | What It Looks At | Example |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Demographic** | Age, income, education, gender | "18-22 year old university students" |
| **Geographic** | Location, climate, urban/rural | "SIT students living >30 min from campus" |
| **Psychographic** | Values, interests, personality | "Students who prioritize healthy eating" |
| **Behavioral** | Usage, buying patterns, brand loyalty | "Students who eat out >5x per week" |

**Pro tip:** The best segments combine 2-3 types. "18-22 year old SIT students who eat out 5x/week and care about health" — this is actionable.

### Slide 6 — B2C vs. B2B: Different Rules
| Dimension | B2C (Consumer) | B2B (Business) |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| Decision maker | Individual | Multiple stakeholders |
| Purchase cycle | Minutes to days | Weeks to months |
| Price sensitivity | High | Low (if value is clear) |
| Relationship | Transactional | Long-term |
| Marketing channel | Social media, ads | LinkedIn, referrals, events |

**Your venture:** Are you B2C, B2B, or both? What changes?

### Slide 7 — Early Adopters vs. Mainstream
- **Early adopters:** Willing to try imperfect products, more forgiving, give feedback
- **Mainstream:** Wait until product is proven, want reliability, less forgiving
- **Strategy:** Find early adopters FIRST. Don't market to mainstream yet.

**The Chasm** (Geoffrey Moore):
```
Early Adopters ─[CHASM]→ Mainstream Market
```
Most startups fail trying to cross this chasm too early.

**Your goal:** Find your early adopters. They're the ones who feel the pain most acutely.

## Part 3: Customer Personas (25 min)

### Slide 9 — What is a Customer Persona?
- **Definition:** A detailed, semi-fictional representation of your ideal customer
- **Not a demographic profile** — it's a *human* with behaviors, motivations, frustrations
- **Why it helps:** Makes your customer real. "Jane, a 19-year-old SIT student who's always hangry" is easier to design for than "students"

**Quote (in their own voice):**

### Slide 11 — Creating Your Persona (15 min)
**Team exercise:**
1. Based on your interviews, describe your target customer as a persona
2. Give them a name, age, and backstory
3. What's their daily routine?
4. When and how do they experience the problem?
5. What do they say when they talk about this problem?

**Tip:** Pull details from your actual interviews. If you interviewed a real person who matches, you can base the persona on them.

**Your job:** Narrow to 3 channels that work for YOUR customer.

### Slide 13 — Bullseye Framework
```
       OUTER RING (19 channels — list all)
    ┌─────────────────────────────────┐
    │     INNER RING                  │
    │  ┌─────────────────────────┐    │
    │  │     BULLSEYE            │    │
    │  │  (1-3 channels to      │    │
    │  │   test FIRST)          │    │
    │  │                        │    │
    │  └─────────────────────────┘    │
    └─────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Process:**
1. Brainstorm: which channels could reach your persona?
2. Rank: which channels have the best "reach × relevance × cost"?
3. Test: try the top 1-3 channels for 2 weeks
4. Evaluate: what worked? Double down. Pivot repeat.

### Slide 15 — Channel Brainstorming Activity (15 min)
**In teams:**
1. List 10 possible channels to reach your persona (3 min)
2. For each channel: rate Reach (1-5), Relevance (1-5), Cost (1=high cost, 5=low cost)
3. Calculate: (Reach × Relevance) / Cost = Channel Score
4. Pick your top 3 channels

**Example:**
| Channel | Reach | Relevance | Cost | Score |
|---------|-------|-----------|------|-------|
| Telegram group | 4 | 5 | 5 | 4.0 |
| TikTok ad | 5 | 3 | 2 | 7.5 |
| Campus booth | 3 | 4 | 4 | 3.0 |

**Top 3:** Telegram groups, TikTok, Instagram stories

## Part 5: Go-to-Market Strategy (15 min)

### Slide 16 — Go-to-Market in One Page
**Your GTM strategy template:**

1. **Target segment:** [who specifically]
2. **Value proposition:** [what we offer, in their words]
3. **Primary channel(s):** [how we reach them]
4. **Customer journey:** [awareness → consideration → purchase]
5. **Revenue approach:** [how they pay]
6. **Success metric:** [how we know it's working]

### Slide 17 — Customer Journey Map
```
Awareness ─→ Consideration ─→ Purchase ─→ Retention ─→ Referral
```

**For each stage:**
- What is the customer thinking?
- What touchpoint do we use?
- What metric do we track?

**Example — Meal-planning app:**
| Stage | Customer Thought | Our Touchpoint | Metric |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|--------|
| Awareness | "I'm always hungry on campus" | Instagram ad | Clicks |
| Consideration | "Could this help me plan meals?" | Landing page | Sign-ups |
| Purchase | "I'll try it for \$3/month" | Payment page | Conversions |
| Retention | "This is saving me time" | Weekly email | Unsubscribe rate |
| Referral | "My friend should try this" | Share link | Invites sent |
