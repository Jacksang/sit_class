# Week 9 — Extension Reading & Enrichment

## Core Reading (Required)

**"Traction: A Startup Guide to Getting Customers"** — Gabriel Weinberg & Justin Mares
- The Bullseye framework for channel testing
- Chapters on the specific channels most relevant to your startup

## Enrichment Readings

### Customer Segmentation
1. **"Crossing the Chasm"** — Geoffrey Moore (Ch 1-3)
   - The classic on technology adoption lifecycle
   - Why early adopters ≠ mainstream customers
   - How to cross the "chasm" between them

2. **"The 22 Immutable Laws of Marketing"** — Ries & Trout
   - The Law of Focus: "The most powerful concept in marketing is owning a word in the prospect's mind"
   - The Law of Sacrifice: "You have to give up something in order to get something"

### Persona Development
3. **"The User's Journey"** — Donna Lichaw
   - Story mapping for customer experience
   - How to create narrative-driven personas

4. **"Jobs to Be Done: Theory to Practice"** — Tony Ulwick
   - Understanding what customers "hire" your product to do
   - Outcome-driven innovation

### Singapore Marketing
5. **"Marketing to Singaporean Consumers"** — WARC (World Advertising Research Center)
   - Cultural nuances: practicality, authority-respecting, digital-first
   - What advertising works in Singapore vs. other markets

6. **"Carousell Marketing Playbook"** — Carousell for Business
   - Practical guide to selling on Carousell
   - Listing optimization, pricing strategies, chat management

### Channel Testing
7. **"How to Find Your First 100 Customers"** — Y Combinator (blog)
   - Do things that don't scale
   - Manual outreach, hand-picked beta users, personal onboarding

8. **"The One-Page Marketing Plan"** — Allan Dib
   - Simple framework for early-stage marketing
   - Focus on one channel at a time
