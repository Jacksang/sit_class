# MIE2001 — 10-Week Syllabus (Condensed)

*Compressed from the original 13-week curriculum. Mid-term and final presentations removed from class time; final Business Plan is a take-home submission. All content remains intact — just leaner delivery.*

---

## Overview

| Original | Condensed | Change |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| 13 weeks | 10 weeks | -3 weeks |
| 7 official topics | 7 official topics | Same topics, compressed schedule |
| Week 7: Midterm Pitch | Absorbed into Week 6 | Teams present progress in-class (10 min informal) |
| Week 13: Final Presentation | **Take-home Business Plan** | No class session — written submission only |
| Weekly reflections | Weekly reflections | Same |

**Assessment structure (updated):**

| Component | Weight | Due |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| Weekly reflections | 20% | Every week |
| Customer Discovery Report | 25% | Week 6 |
| Business Model Canvas | 25% | Week 9 |
| Final Business Plan (take-home) | 30% | Week 10 |

---

## Week-by-Week Outline

### Week 1 — Entrepreneurship Mindset

**Lecture:**
- What is entrepreneurship? (myths vs. reality)
- Growth mindset (Carol Dweck)
- Introduction to Effectuation vs. Causation
- Module roadmap & assessment overview

**Activity:** "Pitch a Bad Idea" icebreaker (20 min)

**Homework:**
- Reflection: "My relationship with entrepreneurship" (250 words)
- Read: "What Makes Entrepreneurs Entrepreneurial?" (Sarasvathy, HBR)

---

### Week 2 — Effectual Entrepreneurship Principles ⭐ *(Interview Demo Focus)*

**Lecture:**
- Recap: causation vs. effectuation (5 min)
- The 5 principles — deep dive (30 min):
  - Bird-in-Hand, Affordable Loss, Crazy Quilt, Lemonade, Pilot-in-the-Plane
- Singapore case study: Carousell or Doctor Anywhere (15 min)

**Activity:** "My Effectual Profile" worksheet + pair share (40 min)

**Homework:**
- Reflection: apply one effectuation principle to a personal decision
- Start forming teams (finalize by Week 3)

---

### Week 3 — Design Thinking & Problem-Finding

**Lecture:**
- Stanford d.school 5-stage model (Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test)
- Design thinking for *business* (not just products)
- Problem-framing: "How might we..." statements

**Activity:** Mini design sprint — solve a campus problem (60 min)

**Homework:**
- Create 3 "How might we..." problem statements
- Read: "Design Thinking" (Tim Brown, HBR)
- **Team formation deadline** — submit team roster

---

### Week 4 — Opportunity Recognition & Customer Discovery

**Lecture:**
- Sources of opportunity (tech shifts, demographics, industry pain points, personal experience)
- Problem-first approach (not solution-first)
- Customer discovery: The Mom Test, interview techniques

**Activity:** Campus scavenger hunt — find 10 frustrations (30 min)

**Homework:**
- Interview 3 people about your top problem assumption
- Read: "The Mom Test" Ch 1-3
- Submit: Opportunity Statement (1-page, team)

---

### Week 5 — Opportunity Assessment & Market Sizing

**Lecture:**
- Market sizing: TAM, SAM, SOM
- Competitive analysis (direct vs. indirect, competitive matrix)
- Feasibility: Go/No-go decision framework

**Activity:** Market sizing workshop — calculate TAM/SAM/SOM for each team's idea (30 min)

**Homework:**
- Interview 5+ people total (ongoing)
- Complete market sizing worksheet
- Prepare Customer Discovery Report (due Week 6)

---

### Week 6 — Lean Startup, MVP & Midterm Check-in

**Lecture:**
- Build-Measure-Learn feedback loop
- MVP design (concierge, Wizard of Oz, landing page, paper prototype)
- Riskiest assumption testing
- *Informal in-class pitch* (10 min per team — progress check, no grade)

**Activity:** MVP Design Sprint — identify riskiest assumption, design cheapest test (30 min)

**Deliverable:**
- **Customer Discovery Report due**
- MVP test plan (prepare for Week 7)

---

### Week 7 — The Startup Team & Choosing Customers

**Lecture (Part 1 — The Startup Team):**
- Co-founder dynamics, equity splits, vesting
- Team charter: roles, decisions, conflict resolution
- Ethics in entrepreneurship

**Lecture (Part 2 — Choosing Customers):**
- Customer segmentation & personas
- Channel selection: Bullseye framework
- Singapore-specific channels (Carousell, Telegram, WhatsApp, Shopee)

**Activity:** Team charter negotiation + Channel brainstorming (40 min)

**Homework:**
- Signed team charter due
- Customer persona + 1-page channel strategy
- Build MVP (start testing)

---

### Week 8 — Revenue Models, Unit Economics & Singapore Funding

**Lecture:**
- Revenue models (transaction, subscription, freemium, advertising, marketplace)
- Unit economics: CAC, LTV, break-even
- Singapore funding landscape (Startup SG, EDG, ACE.SG, angel investors)

**Activity:** Financial model workshop — fill Google Sheets template (45 min)

**Homework:**
- Complete financial model (Google Sheets)
- Write: "How much do I need to start this business?"

---

### Week 9 — Business Model Canvas & Pitching

**Lecture:**
- All 9 blocks of the BMC
- Value Proposition Canvas: pain relievers, gain creators
- Pitch deck structure (Kawasaki's 10/20/30 rule)
- Storytelling & handling Q&A

**Activity:** BMC poster session — gallery walk + sticky-note feedback (45 min)

**Deliverable:**
- **Business Model Canvas due** (1 page + 2-page justification)

**Homework:**
- Refine pitch deck based on feedback
- Begin final Business Plan

---

### Week 10 — Final Business Plan Submission (No Class)

**No lecture. No final presentations.**

**Take-home deliverable — Final Business Plan (due end of week):**

| Section | Pages |
|---------|-------|
| Executive Summary | 1 |
| Problem & Customer Discovery | 1-2 |
| Solution & MVP Results | 1 |
| Business Model Canvas (revised) | 1 |
| Financial Projections | 1 |
| Appendix (interviews, data) | As needed |
| **Total** | **Max 6 pages** |

**Also due:**
- Peer evaluation (confidential, online form)
- Final reflection: "My entrepreneurial journey" (500 words)
