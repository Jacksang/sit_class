## Open Questions for Discussion

### Conceptual Questions
1. **Problem-first vs. technology-first:** Many successful companies (Apple, Tesla) are technology-driven, not problem-driven. Is "problem-first" always the right approach, or are there exceptions?

2. **The Mom Test limits:** What if your target customer genuinely doesn't know they have a problem? (e.g., "I didn't know I needed a smartphone until the iPhone existed"). How do you discover latent needs?

3. **Interview bias:** If you interview 5 friends who think your idea is great, and 5 strangers who are skeptical — who do you believe?

### Application Questions
4. **Frequency vs. Intensity:** Which is more important for a viable business — a problem that happens often (but people tolerate it) or a problem that's rare (but devastating when it happens)?

5. **Your interviews so far:** After your first 1-2 interviews, has your problem hypothesis changed? What surprised you?

6. **Competition as validation:** If you find competitors, is that good news (validates the problem) or bad news (crowded market)?

### Singapore Context Questions
7. Singapore is a small market (5.6M people). Does this change how you think about "opportunity size"? Is a problem with 100,000 Singaporean sufferers worth pursuing?

8. Singaporeans are known for being efficiency-focused and reluctant to pay for convenience (many prefer DIY). How does this affect customer discovery for convenience-based solutions?
