# Week 4 — Extension Reading & Enrichment

## Core Reading (Required)

**"The Mom Test: How to Talk to Customers & Learn If Your Business is a Good Idea When Everyone is Lying to You"** — Rob Fitzpatrick
- Ch 1-3 (available free online: momtestbook.com)
- Practical, concise, indispensable for this module

## Enrichment Readings

### Customer Discovery
1. **"The Mom Test" (Full Book)** — Rob Fitzpatrick
   - Worth reading cover to cover
   - Ch 4-5: How to process what you learn
   - Ch 6-7: Finding people to interview

2. **"The Right It: Why So Many Ideas Fail and How to Make Sure Yours Succeed"** — Alberto Savoia
   - Google's former "Innovation Agitator" on data-driven opportunity assessment
   - Ch 1-3: The importance of "data before deck"

3. **"Talking to Humans"** — Giff Constable (free ebook: talkingtohumans.com)
   - Very short (60 pages), practical guide to customer interviews
   - Interview scripts, question banks, templates

### Opportunity Recognition
4. **"The Innovator's DNA"** — Dyer, Gregersen, Christensen (HBR)
   - Five skills of innovative entrepreneurs: associating, questioning, observing, networking, experimenting
   - How to systematically train yourself to spot opportunities

5. **"Blue Ocean Strategy"** — Kim & Mauborgne (Ch 1-3)
   - Creating new market space vs. competing in existing markets
   - Singapore case: how Singtel created new mobile value

6. **"Where to Play: Finding the Right Market for Your Startup"** — Marc Andreessen (blog)
   - "Market is the most important factor" — why picking the right market matters more than your idea or team

### Singapore Opportunity Sources
7. **"The Future of Singapore's Economy: 10 Growth Sectors"** — EDB Singapore website
   - Sectors Singapore is actively investing in: digital economy, green economy, health & biomedical, advanced manufacturing

8. **"Singapore's Aging Population: Opportunities in Silver Economy"** — DBS Insights (report)
   - Data on spending patterns, healthcare needs, and housing preferences of seniors

## Smart Brevity Version
If you only have 10 minutes:
1. Read "The Mom Test" principle summary (Chapter 1 is 12 pages)
2. Write 3 "good" interview questions for your target user

## Interview Question Bank
| Category | Good Questions |
|----------|---------------|
| Problem discovery | "Tell me about the last time you experienced [situation]" |
| Problem frequency | "How often does this happen? Daily, weekly, monthly?" |
| Current solutions | "What do you do now when this happens?" |
| Pain level | "What's the worst part about this?" |
| Alternative spending | "Have you spent money trying to solve this before?" |
| Willingness to pay | "If a solution existed, what would it need to do for you to use it?" |
