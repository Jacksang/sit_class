## Lab: Customer Interview Practice (45 min)

### Objective
Practice conducting effective customer discovery interviews using The Mom Test principles.

### Part A: Interview Question Design (15 min, individual then pairs)
**Step 1:** Write down your team's problem hypothesis (from end of class)

**Step 2:** For each bad question below, rewrite it as a good (Mom Test) question:

| Bad Question | Your Rewrite |
|--------------|--------------|
| "Would you use an app that helps you find cheaper lunch options near campus?" | |
| "Don't you think finding affordable lunch is frustrating?" | |
| "How much would you pay for a lunch recommendation service?" | |
| "That's a real problem, right?" | |

**Step 3:** Write 3 brand new open-ended questions you could use in your interviews:
1. _______________________________________________
2. _______________________________________________
3. _______________________________________________

**Pair-share (5 min):** Test your questions on a partner. Do they sound natural? Do they avoid leading the answer?

### Part B: Live Interview Practice (20 min, groups of 3)
**Roles:** Interviewer, Interviewee, Observer

**Round 1 (5 min):**
- Interviewee thinks of a real frustration they have as a student
- Interviewer tries to discover: "What's the problem?" — using only good questions
- Observer takes notes: how many times did the interviewer:
  - Pitch their idea? ______
  - Ask a leading question? ______
  - Get a concrete story? ______

**Feedback (2 min):** Observer shares notes. What could the interviewer improve?

**Round 2 (5 min):** Rotate roles. Same process.

**Round 3 (8 min):** Rotate roles again. This time, the "interviewee" is a skeptiical person who doesn't think the problem is real. The interviewer must determine truth from flattery.

## Unit Test: Week 4 Concepts (10 min)

**Name:** _________________  **Date:** _________________

### Part A: Multiple Choice (4 points)

1. The "Mom Test" is about:
   - a) Getting your mom to invest in your startup
   - b) Asking questions that reveal truth even when people want to be nice
   - c) Pitching your idea to friends and family first
   - d) Avoiding interviews because people lie

2. Which of the following is a GOOD customer discovery question?
   - a) "Would you pay \$10/month for a solution to this problem?"
   - b) "Do you think this is a good idea?"
   - c) "Tell me about the last time you had trouble finding parking near campus"
   - d) "If I built this app, would you use it?"

3. Which of these is NOT a common source of business opportunities?
   - a) Technology shifts
   - b) Demographic changes
   - c) Competitor imitation
   - d) Industry inefficiencies

4. The "scratch your own itch" approach means:
   - a) Build the solution you personally need
   - b) Build what investors want
   - c) Copy successful products
   - d) Follow market trends

### Part B: Short Answer (4 points)

5. Name two of the four sources of entrepreneurial opportunity. (2 points)
   - 1. _________________________
   - 2. _________________________

6. Explain in 1-2 sentences: Why is asking "Would you use this?" a bad interview question? (2 points)

### Part C: Application (2 points)

7. Imagine you want to start a business helping SIT students find better internship opportunities. Write TWO good (Mom Test-approved) questions you would ask students in your interviews:

   Q1: _______________________________________________

   Q2: _______________________________________________

---

### Answer Key
1. b
2. c
3. c
4. a
5. Any two: Technology shifts, Demographic changes, Industry inefficiencies, Personal experience
6. People tend to say "yes" to be polite (especially in Singapore culture); it doesn't reveal real behavior
7. Open-ended — check that questions focus on the student's life, not pitching a solution
