## Part 2: Revenue Models (25 min)

### Slide 4 — How Does Your Startup Make Money?
- This is the most important question for any business
- **Key insight:** Your revenue model should match your customer's willingness to pay

### Slide 5 — Common Revenue Models
| Model | How It Works | Example in Singapore |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Transaction** | Per sale commission | Carousell (listing fees + promoted listings) |
| **Subscription** | Recurring fee (monthly/yearly) | Netflix, Spotify, Gym memberships |
| **Freemium** | Free basic + paid premium | Canva, Notion, Telegram Premium |
| **Advertising** | Sell access to users | TikTok, Instagram, Carousell ads |
| **Marketplace** | Take % of each transaction | GrabFood (30% commission), Shopee |
| **Licensing** | Charge for IP use | Software licenses, patents |
| **Affiliate** | Commission for referrals | ShopBack, Klook affiliate |
| **Hardware** | Sell physical products | Apple, Xiaomi, IoT devices |

### Slide 6 — How to Choose a Revenue Model
**Decision framework:**
1. What does your customer expect to pay? (free? per-use? monthly?)
2. What's the competitive norm? (subscription in SaaS, per-item in retail)
3. What's your cost structure? (fixed vs. variable costs)
4. What's easiest to test? (transaction is simplest to validate)

**Student team exercise:** What's the right model for YOUR venture? Why?

### Slide 7 — Pricing Strategy Basics
| Strategy | What It Means | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Cost-plus** | Cost + desired margin | "It costs \$2 to make, sell for \$6" |
| **Value-based** | Price based on perceived value | "Saves you 2 hours/week = worth \$20/month" |
| **Competitor-based** | Similar to competitors | "Grab charges \$3 delivery, so will we" |
| **Penetration** | Low price to gain market share | "First month free, then \$5/month" |

**For students:** Start with competitor-based + value-based. Cost-plus often misprices (undervalues your solution).

## Part 3: Unit Economics — CAC & LTV (25 min)

### Slide 8 — What Are Unit Economics?
- **Definition:** The revenue and cost associated with a single customer
- **Why it matters:** If you can't make money on one customer, you can't make money on 1,000 customers

### Slide 9 — CAC: Customer Acquisition Cost
**Formula:** CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Cost / Number of New Customers

**Example calculation:**
- Instagram ads spend: \$200
- Number of new customers: 20
- CAC = \$200 / 20 = \$10 per customer

**Class exercise:** What's a realistic CAC for your venture in Singapore?
- Facebook/IG ads: \$1-5 per click, 2-10% conversion
- Telegram organic: \$0 (but your time is valuable)
- Referral: \$0-5 (if you offer referral incentives)

### Slide 10 — LTV: Lifetime Value
**Formula:** LTV = Average Revenue Per Customer × Average Customer Lifetime

**Example:**
- Subscription: \$10/month
- Average customer stays: 12 months
- LTV = \$10 × 12 = \$120

### Slide 11 — The Magic Ratio: LTV > 3x CAC
**Rule of thumb:** LTV should be at least 3x CAC

| Ratio | Status |
|-------|--------|
| LTV/CAC < 1 | You lose money on every customer — fix this NOW |
| LTV/CAC 1-3 | Potentially sustainable but need improvement |
| LTV/CAC 3-5 | Healthy business |
| LTV/CAC > 5 | Great business — but check assumptions |

**Example:**
- CAC = \$10, LTV = \$120 → LTV/CAC = 12 → Excellent

**Student exercise:** Calculate your LTV/CAC ratio. If you don't have data yet, make your best estimate and note the assumption.

### Slide 12 — Break-Even Analysis
**Formula:** Break-Even Point = Fixed Costs / (Revenue per Unit - Variable Cost per Unit)

**Example — Bubble tea shop:**
- Monthly fixed costs (rent, salary, utilities): \$8,000
- Price per cup: \$5
- Cost per cup (ingredients, cup): \$1.50
- Unit contribution: \$5 - \$1.50 = \$3.50
- **Break-even:** \$8,000 / \$3.50 = 2,286 cups/month
- That's ~76 cups/day

**Student exercise:** Calculate your break-even. How many customers do you need per month?

## Part 4: Financial Model Workshop (40 min)

### Slide 13 — Financial Model Template
**Google Sheets link:** [link to shared template]

**Template sections:**
1. **Startup costs** — what do you need to spend to launch?
2. **Monthly expenses** — rent, salaries, marketing, hosting, misc
3. **Revenue projections** — customers × price × growth rate
4. **Break-even calculation** — when do you become profitable?
5. **Funding needs** — how much capital until break-even?

### Slide 14 — Filling the Template (35 min)
**Instructor-led walkthrough:**
1. **Startup costs (5 min):** What do you NEED to start? (Not what you WANT)
   - Minimal: \$0-500 (landing page, domain, Canva Pro)
   - Typical student startup: \$100-2,000
2. **Monthly expenses (5 min):** What will you spend every month?
   - Hosting: \$10-50/month
   - Marketing: \$100-500/month
   - Tools: \$20-100/month
3. **Revenue projection (10 min):** Three scenarios
   - Pessimistic: 5 customers in Month 1, 10% monthly growth
   - Realistic: 10 customers in Month 1, 20% monthly growth
   - Optimistic: 20 customers in Month 1, 30% monthly growth
4. **Break-even (5 min):** When do you stop losing money?
5. **Funding needs (10 min):** If you break even in Month 8, how much do you need to survive those 8 months?

### Slide 15 — Metrics to Watch
| Metric | Healthy Signal | Warning Signal |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| Gross margin | >70% (software), >40% (physical goods) | <30% |
| CAC payback period | <6 months | >12 months |
| Monthly burn rate | <70% of revenue | >100% of revenue |
| Revenue growth (monthly) | >15% | <5% |

## Part 5: Singapore Funding Landscape (15 min)

### Slide 16 — Types of Funding
| Type | Amount | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Bootstrapping** | \$0-50K | Personal savings, revenue |
| **Grants** | \$10-100K | Government (Startup SG, EDG) |
| **Angel investment** | \$50-500K | Individual investors |
| **Venture capital** | \$500K-5M+ | VC firms |
| **Incubators** | \$10-50K + mentoring | BLOCK71, Antler, NUS Enterprise |

### Slide 17 — Singapore-Specific Funding Options
| Program | Amount | What It Is |
|---------|--------|------------|
| **Startup SG Founder** | \$50K (matched) | For new founders, includes mentorship |
| **EDG (Enterprise Development Grant)** | Up to 70% of project cost | For business transformation |
| **ACE.SG Grants** | Up to \$30K | Proof-of-concept and market testing |
| **SIT I&E Funding** | Check SIT entrepreneurship office | SIT-specific support |
| **SMU / NUS / NTU grants** | Various | University-specific startup programs |

**Important:** Most grants require you to incorporate a company (ACRA registration). For this module, focus on building a viable business — grants come later.

### Slide 18 — When to Raise vs. When to Bootstrap
| Bootstrap If... | Raise Money If... |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| You can build MVP cheaply | MVP requires significant capital |
| You want to stay in control | You need connections and mentorship |
| Revenue covers your costs | Growth requires hiring/paying |
| You're testing multiple ideas | You're confident in one idea |
| Your market isn't time-sensitive | Speed to market is critical |

**For most student startups:** Bootstrap first. Prove demand. Then consider raising.
