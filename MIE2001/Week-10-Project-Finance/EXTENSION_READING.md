# Week 10 — Extension Reading & Enrichment

## Core Reading (Required)

**"Financial Intelligence for Entrepreneurs"** — Karen Berman & Joe Knight (Ch 1-5)
- Core financial concepts for non-financial founders
- Profit vs. cash flow, revenue recognition, basic financial statements

## Enrichment Readings

### Revenue Models & Pricing
1. **"Monetization: 25 Revenue Models for Digital Products"** — Steli Efti (blog)
   - Practical guide to choosing the right model
   - Pros and cons of each model with real examples

2. **"Don't Just Roll the Dice"** — Neil Davidson (free ebook)
   - Simple guide to software pricing
   - How to run pricing experiments

3. **"Value-Based Pricing for Startups"** — Price Intelligently (blog)
   - How to price based on value, not cost
   - The "Van Westendorp" pricing model

### Unit Economics
4. **"How to Calculate CAC and LTV"** — David Skok (For Entrepreneurs blog)
   - The definitive guide to unit economics for SaaS
   - Downloadable templates

5. **"Why SaaS Startups Die: The Unit Economics Trap"** — Christoph Janz
   - Common mistakes in unit economic calculations
   - Hidden costs that kill startups

### Singapore Financial Context
6. **"The Cost of Doing Business in Singapore"** — DBS Business Insights
   - Real costs: rent, salaries, utilities, licenses
   - Good baseline for financial model assumptions

7. **"Startup SG Founder: Application Guide"** — EnterpriseSG
   - Full details on the \$50K matching grant
   - What reviewers look for

8. **"Singapore Startup Funding Report"** — DealStreetAsia / NUS Enterprise
   - Latest funding data by sector, stage, and amount
   - Good context for your funding recommendation

## Smart Brevity Version
If you only have 10 minutes:
1. Calculate your startup costs (List everything you'd spend in Month 1)
2. Estimate your first customer's CAC (How much to acquire them?)
3. Guess their LTV (How long will they stay × how much will they pay?)
