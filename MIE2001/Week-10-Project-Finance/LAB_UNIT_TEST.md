## Unit Test: Week 10 Concepts (10 min)

**Name:** _________________  **Date:** _________________

### Part A: Multiple Choice (4 points)

1. CAC stands for:
   - a) Customer Acquisition Cost
   - b) Customer Annual Contribution
   - c) Cost of Acquiring Customers
   - d) Customer Average Cost

2. A healthy LTV/CAC ratio is typically:
   - a) < 1x
   - b) 1-2x
   - c) 3x or higher
   - d) > 10x

3. Break-even is when:
   - a) Your company is profitable for the first time
   - b) Your total revenue equals your total costs
   - c) You've paid back your investors
   - d) You have 1,000 customers

4. Which pricing strategy is generally recommended for student startups?
   - a) Cost-plus (add margin to your costs)
   - b) Value-based or competitor-based
   - c) Penetration (lowest price)
   - d) Whatever your competitor charges

### Part B: Short Answer (4 points)

5. Calculate: If your fixed costs are \$2,000/month, revenue per customer is \$20/month, and variable cost per customer is \$5/month, what is your break-even in number of customers? (2 points)

6. Name one Singapore government grant and the maximum amount it provides. (2 points)

### Part C: Application (2 points)

7. Your team's venture has a CAC of \$15 and an LTV of \$45. Is this a healthy ratio? If not, what two things could you do to improve it?

---

### Answer Key
1. a
2. c
3. b
4. b
5. Break-even = 2000 / (20-5) = 133.3 → 134 customers
6. Startup SG Founder (\$50K matching) or EDG (up to 70%) or ACE.SG (up to \$30K)
7. LTV/CAC = 3.0 — borderline healthy (just at 3x). To improve: increase LTV (raise price, increase retention) or decrease CAC (cheaper channels, referral program)
