## Open Questions for Discussion

### Conceptual Questions
1. **Revenue model purity:** Many startups combine models (freemium + ads + marketplace). Is it better to pick one model and be great at it, or combine multiple from Day 1?

2. **CAC assumptions:** For a student startup with \$0 marketing spend (just organic + word of mouth), is CAC = \$0, or should you value your time?

3. **Break-even anxiety:** If break-even is 18 months away, is the startup viable? What's the maximum "time to break-even" a student should accept?

### Application Questions
4. **Your unit economics:** After calculating, does your LTV/CAC ratio look healthy? If not, what would you change?

5. **The \$50K question:** If you were given \$50K (Startup SG Founder grant), what would you spend it on? Would it change your strategy?

6. **Pricing anxiety:** Did you tend to under-price or over-price your solution? Why do founders often under-price?

### Singapore Context Questions
7. Singapore government grants provide up to \$50K matching. Does this make it "too easy" to start a startup? Or is \$50K too little for meaningful progress?

8. COS (Cost of Sales) in Singapore is high — rent, salaries, marketing. Does this mean Singapore startups need higher revenue per customer to survive? How does this affect your business model?
