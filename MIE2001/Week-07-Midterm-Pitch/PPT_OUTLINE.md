## Part 1: Welcome & Instructions (10 min)

### Slide 1 — Title Slide
- **Week 7:** Mid-Term Presentations
- "Where are we now, and what have we learned?"

### Slide 2 — Today's Format
| Segment | Time | What's Happening |
|---------|------|-----------------|
| Pitches | 88 min | 5 min pitch + 3 min Q&A per team |
| Break | 15 min | Fill peer feedback forms |
| Feedback | 15 min | Instructor observations |
| Workshop | 30 min | Team reflection + next steps |
| Discussion | 20 min | Class-wide learnings |

### Slide 3 — Pitch Guidelines
- **5 min strict** (we'll time you — sorry, no grace periods)
- **3 min Q&A** — we'll ask questions, not attack you
- **No polished videos required** — show what you've got (even sketches)
- **All team members** should present at least 1 minute

### Slide 4 — What Judges Are Looking For
| Criteria | Weight | What We Want to See |
|----------|--------|---------------------|
| Problem clarity | 20% | Is the problem real, specific, and validated? |
| Customer evidence | 25% | Real interviews, not assumptions |
| BMC (draft) | 20% | Has thinking about the business model started? |
| MVP progress | 20% | Have you tested something? Even tiny tests count |
| Team & learning | 15% | What have you learned that changed your thinking? |

**Remember:** This is 70% process, 30% outcome. We're grading your learning, not your results.

### Slide 5 — 5-Slide Deck Template (Reminder)
| Slide | Content |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Problem + evidence from interviews |
| 2 | Draft BMC |
| 3 | MVP plan (or results already tested) |
| 4 | Key learnings (what changed, what surprised you) |
| 5 | What you need help with (crowd-sourced advice) |

### Slide 6 — Ground Rules
- Be generous with applause (it's hard to present in front of peers)
- Questions are about curiosity, not gotchas
- Feedback is for the presenter, not for the judge
- If you're not presenting: **put your phone away**, watch, learn

## Part 2: Pitch Block (88 min)

### Running order (adjust based on team count):
- Team 1: 5 + 3 = 8 min
- Team 2: 5 + 3 = 8 min
- Team 3: 5 + 3 = 8 min
- Team 4: 5 + 3 = 8 min
- Team 5: 5 + 3 = 8 min
- Team 6: 5 + 3 = 8 min
- Team 7: 5 + 3 = 8 min
- Team 8: 5 + 3 = 8 min

**(Total: 64 min + 2 min transitions = ~80 min)**

### Instructor Notes (for running the session):
- Use a timer visible to all (project a timer or use phone)
- At 4 min: hold up "1 minute" sign
- At 5 min: stop the pitch (no overruns — fairness to remaining teams)
- After each pitch: 2-3 audience questions, then 1 instructor question
- Take quick notes on each team for individualized feedback

## Part 5: Team Reflection Workshop (30 min)

### Slide 10 — Team Reflection Questions
**In your teams, discuss (then write down):**

1. **What's the most important thing we learned since Week 1?**
   - About our problem?
   - About our customers?
   - About ourselves as a team?

2. **What's one thing we'd do differently if we started over?**

3. **What's our biggest risk going into Weeks 8-13?**

4. **What help do we need most right now?**
   - Technical help (building MVP)?
   - Customer access (finding interviewees)?
   - Business model clarity (BMC)?
   - Team dynamics?

5. **What's our plan for the next 2 weeks?**
   - MVP test
   - Additional interviews
   - Iterating BMC
