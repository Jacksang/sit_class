### Part A: Multiple Choice (6 points)

1. Which effectuation principle is about starting with who you are, what you know, and who you know?
   - a) Affordable Loss
   - b) Crazy Quilt
   - c) Bird-in-Hand
   - d) Pilot-in-the-Plane

2. The correct order of the design thinking stages is:
   - a) Define → Empathize → Ideate → Prototype → Test
   - b) Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test
   - c) Ideate → Empathize → Define → Prototype → Test
   - d) Empathize → Ideate → Define → Test → Prototype

3. "The Mom Test" teaches us to:
   - a) Always ask your mom for startup advice
   - b) Ask questions about people's lives, not about your idea
   - c) Get your mom to invest in your startup
   - d) Avoid talking to anyone who might be biased

4. SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market) is important because it tells you:
   - a) How big the entire industry is
   - b) How much you can realistically capture
   - c) The total number of potential customers worldwide
   - d) What investors expect to see

5. An MVP is best defined as:
   - a) The first version of your product with all core features
   - b) The smallest test of your riskiest assumption
   - c) A polished demo for investors
   - d) A prototype that does everything the final product will do

6. The Build-Measure-Learn loop prioritizes:
   - a) Building as fast as possible
   - b) Minimizing time between building and learning
   - c) Making perfect products
   - d) Getting venture funding
