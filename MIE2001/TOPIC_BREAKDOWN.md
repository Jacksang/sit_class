## Topic 1: Entrepreneurship Mindset & Effectual Entrepreneurship Principles (Weeks 1-2)

**Official LO:** LO1 — Interpret the effectual entrepreneurship principles

### Week 1: What is Entrepreneurship?

**Core question:** What does it mean to be an entrepreneur, and could it be for you?

**Learning Objectives (→ LO1 foundation):**
- Define entrepreneurship beyond "starting a company"
- Distinguish myths from realities
- Introduce effectuation vs. causation
- Understand the module structure

**Lecture Outline (45 min):**
1. Opening: name 5 entrepreneurs — they're all different (5 min)
2. Defining entrepreneurship: Schumpeter's creative destruction → modern problem-solving (10 min)
3. **Growth mindset** (Carol Dweck) — fixed vs. growth, with examples (10 min)
4. **Intro to Effectuation vs. Causation** (Sarasvathy) — the 5 principles at a glance; preview of Week 2 (15 min)
5. Module roadmap (5 min)

**In-Class Activity (30 min):**
**"Pitch a Bad Idea"** — 60-sec pitches of terrible ideas. Class votes on most creative, most likely to accidentally succeed. Debrief: what made even bad ideas compelling?

**Homework:**
- Reflection: "My relationship with entrepreneurship" (250 words)
- Read: "What Makes Entrepreneurs Entrepreneurial?" (Sarasvathy, HBR)

### Week 2: Effectual Entrepreneurship Principles

**Core question:** How do expert entrepreneurs think differently?

**Learning Objectives (→ LO1):**
- Explain all 5 effectuation principles with examples
- Distinguish effectuation from causation
- Apply effectuation to personal decisions and venture ideas

**Lecture Outline (45 min):**
1. Recap: causation vs. effectuation (5 min)
2. **5 Principles deep dive** (25 min):
   - Bird-in-Hand: start with who you are, what you know, whom you know
   - Affordable Loss: invest only what you can afford to lose
   - Crazy Quilt: build partnerships, don't compete
   - Lemonade: embrace surprises, turn bad news into opportunity
   - Pilot-in-the-Plane: focus on what you can control
3. Case study: identify effectuation principles in a Singapore startup (15 min)

**In-Class Activity (30 min):**
**"My Effectual Profile" — Personal worksheet:**
- Bird-in-Hand: List your identity, knowledge, and network
- Affordable Loss: What can you afford to lose (time, money, ego)?
- Crazy Quilt: Who could you partner with?
- Lemonade: Describe a recent surprise — how could it be an opportunity?
- Share in pairs, discuss insights

**Homework:**
- Reflection: apply one effectuation principle to a decision you made this week
- Read: effectuation case study (Singapore startup)

## Topic 2: Design Thinking Procedures (Week 3)

**Official LO:** LO2 — Apply creative problem-solving and critical thinking

### Week 3: Design Thinking for Entrepreneurs

**Core question:** How do you solve problems the way designers do?

**Learning Objectives (→ LO2):**
- Explain the 5 stages of design thinking
- Apply design thinking to business problems
- Use design thinking to generate venture ideas

**Lecture Outline (45 min):**
1. Design thinking overview: Stanford d.school 5-stage model (10 min)
   - Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test
2. Design thinking for business, not just products (10 min)
   - Case: Airbnb, Apple, or a Singapore example
   - How DT complements lean startup
3. Problem-framing: "How might we..." statements (10 min)
4. From problem to opportunity: the entrepreneur's lens (15 min)

**In-Class Activity (45 min):**
**Mini Design Sprint (90 min total — extended class or two sessions):**
1. Empathize: observe a campus problem area (10 min)
2. Define: create "How might we..." statements (10 min)
3. Ideate: brainstorm 20+ solutions (10 min)
4. Prototype: sketch the best 1-2 solutions (10 min)
5. Share (5 min)

**Homework:**
- Create 3 "How might we..." problem statements for potential ventures
- Read: "Design Thinking" (Tim Brown, HBR)

## Topic 3: Opportunity Recognition & Assessment (Weeks 4-5)

**Official LO:** LO3 — Identify opportunities and assess feasibility

### Week 4: Opportunity Recognition & Customer Discovery

**Core question:** How do you find problems worth solving?

**Learning Objectives (→ LO3):**
- Apply systematic opportunity recognition
- Design customer discovery interviews
- Distinguish validated problems from assumptions

**Lecture Outline (45 min):**
1. Sources of opportunity (15 min):
   - Technology shifts (AI, blockchain, sustainability)
   - Demographic changes (aging population, gig economy)
   - Industry inefficiencies (fragmented markets, regulation)
   - Personal experience (scratch your own itch)
2. The Problem-First approach (10 min):
   - Problems → solutions → business (not the reverse)
   - The "Napkin Test": is there a problem? A market? Can you build it?
3. Customer discovery introduction (20 min):
   - The Mom Test principles
   - Open-ended questions that get real answers
   - Interviewing: who, when, how many

**In-Class Activity (30 min):**
**"Campus Scavenger Hunt"** — walk around campus in pairs, find 10 frustrations/inefficiencies. Share top 3 in class.

**Homework:**
- Interview 3 people about your top problem assumption
- Read: "The Mom Test" Ch 1-3

### Week 5: Opportunity Assessment — Market Sizing & Feasibility

**Core question:** Is this opportunity worth pursuing?

**Learning Objectives (→ LO3 continued):**
- Calculate TAM/SAM/SOM for any business idea
- Conduct basic competitive analysis
- Make a go/no-go decision on an idea

**Lecture Outline (45 min):**
1. Market sizing fundamentals (20 min):
   - TAM: total addressable market
   - SAM: serviceable available market
   - SOM: serviceable obtainable market
   - Singapore examples: bubble tea shop vs. SaaS
2. Competitive assessment (15 min):
   - Direct vs. indirect competitors
   - Competitive matrix (price, features, positioning)
   - Blue Ocean vs. Red Ocean
3. Feasibility: when to pursue vs. when to pivot (10 min)

**In-Class Activity (30 min):**
**Market sizing workshop** — groups calculate TAM/SAM/SOM for each other's ideas. Compare results. Identify which have "escape velocity."

**Homework:**
- Complete interview 5+ people total
- Market sizing worksheet
- Submit: **Customer Discovery Report** — interview notes + problem validation (due Week 7)

## Topic 4: Lean Startup Principles, MVP, and the Startup Team (Weeks 6-8)

**Official LO:** LO2, LO3, LO4

### Week 6: Lean Startup & MVP Design

**Core question:** What's the smallest thing you can build to start learning?

**Learning Objectives (→ LO2, LO3):**
- Explain the Build-Measure-Learn feedback loop
- Design a Minimum Viable Product
- Identify and test the riskiest assumption

**Lecture Outline (45 min):**
1. The Lean Startup philosophy (15 min):
   - Build-Measure-Learn loop
   - Validated learning
   - Case: Zappos (concierge MVP), Dropbox (video MVP)
2. What is an MVP? (15 min):
   - NOT a prototype — it's a learning vehicle
   - Types: landing page, concierge, Wizard of Oz, paper prototype
   - Singapore example: local startup's MVP
3. Riskiest assumption testing (15 min):
   - How to identify your riskiest assumption
   - Design an experiment to test it
   - Success criteria: what would convince you?

**In-Class Activity (30 min):**
**MVP Design Sprint** — each team: identify riskiest assumption → design cheapest MVP → sketch it → define success criteria

**Homework:**
- Build MVP (landing page, paper prototype, or simplest version)
- Test with 5-10 potential users
- Read: "The Lean Startup" Ch 6-8

### Week 7: Mid-term Presentations

**Format:** 5-min pitch per team + 3 min Q&A (2 hours total)

**What to present:**
1. Problem + customer discovery results (2 min)
2. Draft BMC (1 min)
3. MVP plan (1 min)
4. Key learning so far (1 min)

**Deliverable:** 5-slide pitch deck + draft BMC

### Week 8: The Startup Team

**Core question:** Who do you need, and how do you work together?

**Learning Objectives (→ LO4):**
- Understand co-founder dynamics and equity splits
- Create a team charter for healthy collaboration
- Recognize ethical dilemmas in startups

**Lecture Outline (45 min):**
1. Why investors bet on teams (10 min):
   - Data: team quality > idea quality
   - SIT alumni founder example
2. Equity & co-founder dynamics (15 min):
   - Splitting equity fairly (not always equally)
   - Vesting schedules
   - What to look for in a co-founder
3. Team charter (10 min):
   - Roles and responsibilities
   - Decision-making process
   - Conflict resolution
4. Ethics in entrepreneurship (10 min):
   - Ethical decision-making frameworks
   - Singapore-specific: regulatory compliance, IP
   - Case: ethical dilemma in a real startup

**In-Class Activity (30 min):**
**Team charter negotiation** — Before class: each member fills out "team values" worksheet. In class: negotiate and sign team charter.

**Homework:**
- Submit signed team charter
- Reflection: "What makes a startup team great?"
- Read: case study on a successful co-founder story

## Topic 5: Choosing Customers (Week 9)

**Official LO:** LO4 — Demonstrate entrepreneurial leadership and communication skills

### Week 9: Customer Segmentation & Go-to-Market

**Core question:** Who is your first customer, and how do you reach them?

**Learning Objectives (→ LO4):**
- Segment customers and create personas
- Choose the right go-to-market channels
- Design a channel strategy for Singapore

**Lecture Outline (45 min):**
1. Customer segmentation (15 min):
   - Demographic, psychographic, behavioral
   - B2C vs. B2B
   - Early adopters vs. mainstream
2. Creating customer personas (10 min):
   - What makes a good persona?
   - Template exercise
3. Channel selection (20 min):
   - Direct sales, online, partnerships, content
   - Bullseye framework (test channels systematically)
   - **Singapore-specific channels:** Carousell, Telegram groups, WhatsApp broadcasts, TikTok SG, Shopee/Lazada

**In-Class Activity (30 min):**
**Channel brainstorming:** each team identifies 10 possible channels to reach their target customer. Rank by reach × relevance × cost. Select top 3.

**Homework:**
- Create 1 customer persona
- Write 1-page channel strategy
- Read: Traction (Gabriel Weinberg) — Bullseye Framework overview

## Topic 6: Project Finance & Pitching to Investors (Weeks 10-11)

**Official LO:** LO4, LO5

### Week 10: Revenue Models, Unit Economics, & Singapore Funding

**Core question:** How does your startup make money, and how much do you need?

**Learning Objectives (→ LO5):**
- Choose a revenue model for a venture
- Calculate basic unit economics
- Understand funding options in Singapore
- Build a simple financial model

**Lecture Outline (45 min):**
1. Revenue models (15 min):
   - Transaction, subscription, freemium, advertising, marketplace
   - Case examples: Grab (transaction) vs. Netflix (subscription) vs. Canva (freemium)
2. Unit economics (15 min):
   - CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
   - LTV (Lifetime Value)
   - The magic ratio: LTV > 3x CAC
   - Break-even analysis
3. Singapore funding landscape (15 min):
   - **Grants:** Startup SG Founder ($50k matching), EDG
   - **Investors:** Angels vs. VCs vs. grants
   - **Bootstrapping vs. fundraising:** the dilution trade-off
   - When to raise vs. when to bootstrap

**In-Class Activity (45 min):**
**Financial model workshop** — use provided Google Sheets template. Fill in: startup costs, monthly expenses, revenue projections. Calculate break-even.

**Homework:**
- Complete financial model in Google Sheets
- Write: "How much do I need to start this business, and where will that money come from?"
- Read: Singapore startup bootstrapping case study

### Week 11: Pitching to Investors

**Core question:** How do you tell your story in a way that makes people want to invest?

**Learning Objectives (→ LO4, LO5):**
- Structure a compelling pitch deck
- Master storytelling for business
- Handle Q&A confidently

**Lecture Outline (45 min):**
1. The pitch deck structure (15 min):
   - 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30pt font (Kawasaki's rule)
   - Problem → Solution → Market → Traction → Team → Financials → Ask
   - What investors actually look for
2. Storytelling (15 min):
   - Data with narrative
   - Emotional connection + logical argument
   - The "elevator pitch" formula
3. Handling Q&A (15 min):
   - Anticipating tough questions
   - "I don't know" is acceptable (shows curiosity)
   - Common investor questions

**In-Class Activity (30 min):**
**Pitch practice:** 5-min pitch per team. Class gives structured feedback (2 stars + 1 wish). Video record for self-review.

**Homework:**
- Refine pitch deck
- Video record self, self-critique
- Practice without notes
