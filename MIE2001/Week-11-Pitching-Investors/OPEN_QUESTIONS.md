## Open Questions for Discussion

### Conceptual Questions
1. **Authenticity in pitching:** Should you be 100% transparent about risks and uncertainties in a pitch, or is some amount of gloss acceptable?

2. **Demo vs. Deck:** For a 7-minute pitch, is it better to show a live demo (risky — might fail) or a recorded demo (safe — less impressive)?

3. **The "perfect" pitch deck:** Is there such a thing? Or is every pitch different depending on the audience?

### Application Questions
4. **Your pitch experience:** After today's practice, what's the ONE thing you'll change before next week?

5. **Q&A readiness:** What's the hardest question someone asked you today? Could you answer it better now?

6. **Story vs. Data:** Which part of your pitch feels strongest — the emotional story or the logical data? Which do you need to improve?

### Singapore Context Questions
7. In Singapore, pitch competitions are common (SLINGSHOT, NUS Startup Weekend). How does pitching for investment differ from pitching for a competition?

8. Singapore investors are often described as "conservative" — they want proven traction before investing. How does this affect how you structure your pitch compared to a Silicon Valley pitch?
