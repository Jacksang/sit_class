# Week 11 — Lab & Unit Test Design

## Lab: Pitch Practice & Feedback (50 min)

### Objective
Deliver a practice pitch, receive structured feedback, and refine before finals.

### Part A: Elevator Pitch Warm-Up (10 min, pairs)
- **Round 1 (3 min):** Deliver your 30-second elevator pitch. Partner gives one "strong" and one "improve"
- **Round 2 (3 min):** Switch. Same format.
- **Round 3 (4 min):** Partner plays "skeptical investor" — you must convince them

## Unit Test: Week 11 Concepts (10 min)

**Name:** _________________  **Date:** _________________

### Part A: Multiple Choice (4 points)

1. Guy Kawasaki's 10/20/30 rule refers to:
   - a) 10 minutes, 20 slides, 30 second intro
   - b) 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30-point font
   - c) 10 questions, 20 answers, 30 seconds each
   - d) 10 key points, 20 data points, 30 images

2. In a pitch, the "hero" should be:
   - a) The founder
   - b) The product
   - c) The customer
   - d) The investor

3. The most important slide in a pitch deck is typically:
   - a) The title slide
   - b) The problem slide (Slide 2) — it sets up everything
   - c) The team slide
   - d) The financials slide

4. When an investor asks "What's your biggest risk?", you should:
   - a) Say there are no risks
   - b) Minimize the risk
   - c) Name a real risk AND how you'll address it
   - d) Change the subject

### Part B: Short Answer (4 points)

5. Why is "Show, don't tell" important for the solution slide? (2 points)

6. What's the purpose of the "Ask" slide? What should it include? (2 points)
