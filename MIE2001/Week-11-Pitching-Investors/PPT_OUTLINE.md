## Part 2: The Pitch Deck (30 min)

### Slide 4 — The Purpose of a Pitch Deck
- **NOT** to explain everything about your business
- **YES** to make them *want* to learn more
- **Guy Kawasaki's 10/20/30 Rule:** 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30-point font
- For our purposes: **10 slides max, 7 minutes**

### Slide 5 — The 10-Slide Structure
| Slide | Content |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Title + Tagline (1 sentence) |
| 2 | The Problem (specific, emotional) |
| 3 | The Solution (your product/service) |
| 4 | Market Size (TAM → SAM → SOM) |
| 5 | Traction (what you've proven so far) |
| 6 | Business Model (how you make money) |
| 7 | Competition (unique advantage) |
| 8 | Team (why YOU can do this) |
| 9 | Financials (revenue projection, break-even) |
| 10 | The Ask (what you need + what you'll achieve) |

### Slide 6 — Slide-by-Slide Breakdown

**Slide 1: Title + Tagline**
- Company name, logo, one-line tagline
- "The Airbnb of [industry]" — BUT only if it's genuinely accurate
- **Example:** "Helping SIT students find affordable lunch in under 3 minutes"

**Slide 2: The Problem**
- Start with a story or statistic
- Make the pain tangible
- **Example:** "The average SIT student spends 15 min per day deciding where to eat. That's 60 hours per semester lost."

**Slide 3: The Solution**
- Show, don't tell (screenshots, demo video, prototype photos)
- How it works in 3 steps
- **Key:** Focus on benefits, not features

**Slide 4: Market Size**
- TAM → SAM → SOM (learned in Week 5)
- Visual: the three-ring diagram
- Focus on SOM — show you're realistic

**Slide 5: Traction**
- Customer interviews count. MVP results count.
- "Interviewed 15 students. 12 confirmed the problem. 8 pre-registered."
- If you have revenue: SHOW IT (even if it's \$50)

**Slide 6: Business Model**
- How do you make money?
- Pricing, CAC, LTV
- Keep it simple

**Slide 7: Competition**
- Direct + Indirect matrix
- Your unique advantage (why not just use status quo?)

**Slide 8: Team**
- Photos + 1-line bio per member
- Why is THIS team uniquely suited?
- **Hint:** Mention complementary skills

**Slide 9: Financials**
- 3-year revenue projection (simplified)
- Break-even point
- Funding needed to get there

**Slide 10: The Ask**
- "We're raising \$XXX to achieve [milestone] by [date]"
- OR "We're seeking [mentorship/partnership/introduction] to [goal]"
- Clear, specific, achievable

### Slide 7 — Good Deck vs. Bad Deck (Show Examples)
| Bad Deck | Good Deck |
|----------|-----------|
| 20+ slides | 10 slides |
| Tiny text | 30+ pt font |
| Lots of words | Mostly visuals |
| No flow | Clear story arc |
| "We're a platform..." | "We help [customer]..." |
| No traction shown | Real data, even small |

**Activity (5 min):** Show a terrible pitch deck (or slide) and ask: "What would you change?"

## Part 3: Storytelling (20 min)

### Slide 8 — Data Without Story is Boring; Story Without Data is Fiction
- **The formula:** Emotional hook + Logical argument = Compelling pitch
- **Structure:** Beginning (problem) → Middle (journey to solution) → End (future vision)

### Slide 9 — The Hero's Journey for Pitches
```
Customer lives normally → encounters a problem (the villain) 
→ searches for solutions → finds YOUR solution → life is better
```

**Your job:** Position the CUSTOMER as the hero. You're the guide/mentor.

**Example:**
- **Hero:** The busy SIT student who's always hungry
- **Villain:** Decision fatigue from too many food options
- **Guide:** Your app
- **Plan:** Uses filters + reviews to find the perfect option in 30 seconds
- **Happy ending:** Student saves time, eats better, is less hangry

### Slide 10 — Storytelling Workshop (10 min)
**Team exercise:** Write a 3-sentence pitch story

1. **The hook:** "Before [solution], [customer] struggled with [problem]..."
2. **The journey:** "We built [solution] to solve this..."
3. **The vision:** "Now, [customer] can [benefit]..."

**Example:**
> "Before LunchMate, SIT students wasted 15 minutes every day figuring out where to eat. We built a simple app that shows you the best lunch options near campus in 30 seconds, based on what you actually want. Now, students save 60 hours per semester — time they can spend studying, working, or just relaxing."

## Part 4: Handling Q&A (15 min)

### Slide 11 — Common Investor Questions
| Question | What They're Really Asking | How to Answer |
|----------|---------------------------|---------------|
| "Why will this work?" | What evidence do you have? | Share your traction/validation |
| "Who else is doing this?" | Do you understand the competition? | Show competitive matrix |
| "How will you make money?" | Is there a real business? | Show unit economics |
| "What's your biggest risk?" | Are you honest about challenges? | Name it AND show how you'll address it |
| "Why YOUR team?" | Do you have the right skills? | Show complementary expertise |
| "What if you fail?" | Are you resilient? | "We'd learn and pivot" — with specifics |
| "How much do you need?" | Do you know what you're doing? | Clear, justified number |

### Slide 12 — Handling Tough Questions
1. **Don't lie or exaggerate** — investors can smell it
2. **"I don't know" is OK** — follow with "but I'll find out"
3. **Redirect to your strength** — "That's an interesting question. What we've focused on so far is X..."
4. **Practice the 5 hardest questions** before your pitch

### Slide 13 — Q&A Role-Play (10 min)
**In teams of 3:** 
- Presenter: gives 1-min pitch
- Investor 1: asks a tough question
- Investor 2: asks another tough question
- Observer: takes notes on strengths and areas to improve

**Rotate so everyone practices at least once.**

## Part 5: Pitch Practice + Feedback (40 min)

### Slide 14 — Pitch Practice: 5-Min Format
**Each team gets 5 min to present (full deck, not truncated)**

- **Audience feedback (structured):**
  - 2 things that worked well
  - 1 thing to improve
  - 1 question I still have

- **Instructor feedback:**
  - Timing check (over/under?)
  - Clarity score (1-10)
  - Biggest improvement area

**Schedule (adjust for team count):**
- Team 1: 5 min pitch + 3 min feedback
- Team 2: 5 min pitch + 3 min feedback
- Team 3: 5 min pitch + 3 min feedback
- Team 4: 5 min pitch + 3 min feedback
- Team 5: 5 min pitch + 3 min feedback

### Slide 15 — Video Self-Critique
- **Record your pitch** (phone is fine)
- **Watch it back tonight**
- **Ask yourself:**
  - Did I look at the audience or my slides?
  - Did I speak clearly and at a good pace?
  - Did I sound excited about the problem?
  - Where did I hesitate or rush?

## Open Discussion Block (15 min)

1. **Pitch anxiety:** What's the most nerve-wracking part of presenting? How do you deal with it?
2. **"The Ask":** For a student startup, is the "ask" always for funding? Or are there more valuable things to ask for?
3. **Faking confidence:** Should you pretend to be more confident than you feel, or is vulnerability more compelling?
4. **Pitch deck design:** How much does visual design matter vs. the content? Is a polished Canva deck worth the time?
