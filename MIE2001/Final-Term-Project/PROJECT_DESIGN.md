# Final Term Project: Venture Pitch & Business Plan

## Overview

**Weight:** Pitch Deck + Presentation = 25%, BMC = 20% of final grade  
**Due:** Week 13 (presentation day)  
**Format:** 10-slide pitch deck (PDF) + Business plan (5 pages max, group)  
**GOAL:** Present a compelling, evidence-backed venture to a panel of judges

---

## Project Objective

By Week 13, each team must deliver:
1. A **7-minute investor pitch** (to a panel of instructors + guest judges)
2. A **business plan** (executive summary + final BMC + 1-page financials)

This project integrates EVERYTHING you've learned this semester: problem validation, customer discovery, MVP testing, business model design, financial modeling, and pitching.

---

## Deliverable 1: Pitch Deck (10 slides max, PDF)

### Slide-by-Slide Requirements

| Slide # | Content | Key Elements |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **Title Slide** | Company name, tagline (one sentence), team names |
| 2 | **Problem** | Specific problem, who has it, evidence from interviews |
| 3 | **Solution** | Your product/service, how it works (show, don't tell) |
| 4 | **Market Size** | TAM → SAM → SOM (with numbers and sources) |
| 5 | **Traction** | What have you validated? Interviews, MVP results, pre-signups |
| 6 | **Business Model** | How you make money, pricing, unit economics (CAC, LTV) |
| 7 | **Competition** | Direct + indirect + your unique advantage |
| 8 | **Team** | Photos + bios + why THIS team can execute |
| 9 | **Financials** | Revenue projection, break-even, funding needs |
| 10 | **The Ask** | What you need + what you'll achieve with it |

### Design Guidelines
- **Font:** Minimum 24pt (30+ recommended)
- **Visuals:** More images/visuals than text (screenshots, sketches, data visualizations)
- **Consistency:** Same color scheme, fonts, and branding throughout
- **File format:** PDF (to avoid formatting issues on the judging panel's devices)

## Deliverable 2: Business Plan (5 pages max)

### Structure

#### Page 1: Executive Summary (½ page)
- The venture in 3-4 sentences
- Problem, solution, target customer, business model
- Current status (what has been validated)
- Funding ask (if applicable)

#### Page 2: Business Model Canvas (1 page)
- Final 9-block BMC (visual format — can be image)
- Each block clearly filled with specific content (not generic)

#### Page 3: BMC Justification (1 page)
- **Customer Segments:** Who specifically? Why this segment? How did you arrive at this?
- **Value Proposition:** What value do you provide? Evidence from interviews that this matters?
- **Channels:** Top 3 channels + why they work for this segment
- **Customer Relationships:** How do you acquire, retain, and grow customers?

#### Page 4: BMC Justification (continued) (1 page)
- **Revenue Streams:** Revenue model choice + pricing logic + unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback period)
- **Key Resources:** What do you need that's unique or critical?
- **Key Activities:** What do you spend most of your time on?
- **Key Partnerships:** Who helps you deliver value?
- **Cost Structure:** Fixed vs. variable costs, break-even analysis

#### Page 5: Financial Summary (1 page)
- 3-year revenue projection (simplified)
- Key metrics: CAC, LTV, LTV/CAC ratio, break-even timeline
- Funding needs (if applicable): how much, where it goes, milestone achieved

## Grading Rubric

### Pitch Deck + Presentation (25% of final grade)

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Satisfactory (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Problem & Validation (20%)** | Clear, specific problem with strong interview evidence | Good problem, some evidence | Vague problem, limited evidence | No clear problem |
| **Solution & MVP (15%)** | Solution clearly addresses problem; MVP tested with results | Solution fits; MVP at least designed | Solution solution-prescribed; MVP theoretical | No clear solution or MVP |
| **Business Model (20%)** | Complete BMC; clear revenue model; healthy unit economics | Mostly complete BMC; some revenue thinking | Partial BMC; unclear revenue | Incomplete BMC; no revenue model |
| **Market & Competition (15%)** | Realistic market sizing; clear competitive advantage | Reasonable sizing; some competition awareness | Vague sizing; no competitor analysis | No market or competition |
| **Team & Storytelling (15%)** | Compelling narrative; clear team fit; confident delivery | Good story; decent delivery | Adequate but not memorable | Weak story or delivery |
| **Q&A Handling (15%)** | Clear, honest answers; acknowledges unknowns gracefully | Answers most questions well | Struggles with tough questions | Defensive or unprepared |

### Business Model Canvas (20% of final grade)

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Satisfactory (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Completeness** | All 9 blocks filled, specific content | 8-9 blocks, minor gaps | 5-7 blocks | <5 blocks |
| **Coherence** | Blocks tell consistent story | Most blocks align | Some contradictions | Blocks don't fit |
| **Evidence-based** | Each block justified by research | Most blocks justified | Some claims unsupported | No research basis |
| **Iteration** | Clear evolution from Week 7 draft | Some changes made | Minor changes | No iteration |

## Notes for Judges

- Evaluated on **process** (interviews, iterations, learning) as much as **outcome** (business viability)
- A "failed" venture with deep learning is higher quality than a "successful" one with no learning
- Look for evidence of iteration: how did their thinking change from Week 7 to Week 13?
- Team dynamics matter — did all members contribute to the pitch?
- Reward honesty: "We tested our assumption and it was wrong" is impressive, not a weakness
