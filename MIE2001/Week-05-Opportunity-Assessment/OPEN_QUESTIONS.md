## Open Questions for Discussion

### Conceptual Questions
1. **TAM temptation:** Many founders inflate their TAM to impress investors ("We're going after a \$500B market!"). At what point does a big TAM become a red flag instead of a green flag?

2. **"No direct competitors" — good or bad?** If nobody is doing what you're doing, does that mean you're a genius or that you're solving a non-existent problem?

3. **Go/No-Go at what stage?** If a student idea scores 4/10 on the feasibility assessment, should they pivot now or keep exploring? At what point do you cut your losses?

### Application Questions
4. **Interview truth vs. flattery:** You've interviewed 5 people. 4 said "great idea!" but showed no real commitment. 1 was skeptical but spent 30 minutes giving detailed feedback. Whose opinion matters more?

5. **Your market sizing:** After calculating your SOM, does your idea feel more or less viable than before? Did the number surprise you?

6. **Blue ocean vs. red ocean:** Is "blue ocean" truly possible for student startups, or are most student ideas competing in existing spaces?

### Singapore Context Questions
7. Singapore government grants (Startup SG, EDG) require a certain market potential. Does the availability of grants encourage startups to inflate their market size to qualify? What are the consequences of this?

8. In Singapore's small market, competitive pressure is high. Is it better to build a "Singapore-first, then expand" strategy, or should students think about SEA markets from Day 1?
