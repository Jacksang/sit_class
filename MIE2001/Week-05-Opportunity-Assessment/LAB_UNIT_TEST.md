# Week 5 — Lab & Unit Test Design

## Lab: Market Sizing & Competitive Analysis Workshop (45 min)

### Objective
Calculate TAM/SAM/SOM for your idea and map your competitive landscape.

### Part A: TAM / SAM / SOM Calculator (20 min, teams)

**Step 1:** Estimate your customer base

| Tier | Number of customers | Annual spend per customer | Total |
|------|-------------------|--------------------------|-------|
| **TAM** | Total potential worldwide: ________ | × \$_______ = | \$________ |
| **SAM** | Total in your reachable market: ________ | × \$_______ = | \$________ |
| **SOM** | Realistically capturable (first 1-3 yrs): ________ | × \$_______ = | \$________ |

**Step 2:** Check your assumptions
- How did you get your "number of customers"? (top-down from published data? bottom-up from observation?)
- Is your annual spend estimate based on real data or guessing?
- If SOM < \$100K/year: is this a project or a business?

**Step 3:** Reality check
- Find an existing company in a similar space. Look up their actual revenue. How does it compare to your estimate?
- If you can't find actual data: what's a conservative version of your estimate? (Halve your assumptions. Still viable?)

## Unit Test: Week 5 Concepts (10 min)

**Name:** _________________  **Date:** _________________

### Part A: Multiple Choice (4 points)

1. TAM stands for:
   - a) Total Available Market
   - b) Total Addressable Market
   - c) Target Addressable Margin
   - d) True Addressable Market

2. SOM is important because it tells you:
   - a) The total global opportunity
   - b) How much you can realistically capture as a specific business
   - c) What investors are willing to pay for your company
   - d) The total value of competitors in your space

3. An "indirect competitor" is:
   - a) A company you compete with but don't talk to
   - b) A company that solves the same problem with a different solution
   - c) A company that solves a different problem but targets the same customer
   - d) A company that could become a competitor in the future

4. "Blue Ocean Strategy" is about:
   - a) Competing in crowded markets with better execution
   - b) Creating new market space where competition is irrelevant
   - c) Focusing on ocean-related business opportunities
   - d) Copying successful competitors with slight improvements

### Part B: Short Answer (4 points)

5. Calculate: If TAM is \$10B and your SAM is \$50M, but your SOM is only \$200K, is this a viable business? Why or why not? (2 points)

6. What's the difference between direct and indirect competition? Give one example of each. (2 points)

### Part C: Application (2 points)

7. Your team's idea: ___________________________________

Write a brief assessment of your go/no-go decision. Include what score you gave each factor and your final decision (pursue, pivot, or abandon).

### Answer Key
1. b
2. b
3. b
4. b
5. Depends — \$200K SOM may be enough for a lifestyle business (1-2 people) but not a VC-funded startup; key is whether the SOM can grow
6. Direct = same problem, same solution (e.g., GrabFood vs. Foodpanda). Indirect = same problem, different solution (e.g., GrabFood vs. cooking at home)
7. Open-ended — accept any reasoned assessment
