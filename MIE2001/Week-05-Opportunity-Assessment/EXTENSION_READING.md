# Week 5 — Extension Reading & Enrichment

## Core Reading (Required)

**"The Right It: Why So Many Ideas Fail and How to Make Sure Yours Succeeds"** — Alberto Savoia (Ch 1-3)
- Data-first approach to testing assumptions before building
- Available at SIT library or Google Books

## Enrichment Readings

### Market Sizing
1. **"How to Size a Market"** — Dave Parker (blog, Venture Hacks)
   - Practical step-by-step for early-stage startups
   - Top-down vs. bottom-up approaches with examples

2. **"Market Sizing for Startups: A Guide"** — CB Insights
   - How VCs calculate market size (and common mistakes founders make)
   - Warning: "TAM too large" is a red flag

3. **"Slicing the Pie: TAM, SAM, SOM Explained"** — Tomasz Tunguz
   - Why startups should focus on SOM first, not TAM
   - Case: how Atlassian started with a tiny SOM

### Competitive Analysis
4. **"Blue Ocean Strategy"** — W. Chan Kim & Renée Mauborgne (HBR, 2004)
   - Original HBR article (shorter than the book)
   - The strategy canvas: visual tool for competitive positioning

5. **"Competitive Strategy"** — Michael Porter (Ch 1-3)
   - The five forces that shape industry competition
   - Classic framework, still relevant

6. **"How to Map Your Competitive Landscape"** — First Round Review
   - Practical workshop-style guide for startups
   - Template for competitive matrix

### Feasibility & Go/No-Go
7. **"The Hard Thing About Hard Things"** — Ben Horowitz (Ch 1)
   - When to pursue vs. when to pivot
   - Honest look at startup decision-making

8. **"The Art of the Start 2.0"** — Guy Kawasaki (Ch on getting started)
   - The "Top 10 Lies of Entrepreneurs" (great reality check)
   - How to prototype without coding
