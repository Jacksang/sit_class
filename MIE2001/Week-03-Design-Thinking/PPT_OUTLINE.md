## Part 2: Design Thinking Overview (20 min)

### Slide 4 — What is Design Thinking?
- **Definition:** A human-centered, iterative approach to problem-solving
- **Why it matters for entrepreneurs:** It's a systematic way to find problems worth solving
- **Key difference from traditional problem-solving:** You solve FOR people, not FOR the business

### Slide 5 — The 5-Stage Model (d.school)
```
EMPATHIZE → DEFINE → IDEATE → PROTOTYPE → TEST
```
- **Empathize:** Understand the user's experience and motivation
- **Define:** Frame the right problem to solve
- **Ideate:** Brainstorm a wide range of solutions
- **Prototype:** Build a quick, low-fidelity version
- **Test:** Get feedback from real users

**Visual:** Show the iteration arrow — it's NOT a straight line. You go back and forth.

### Slide 6 — Why Entrepreneurs Need Design Thinking
- **Without DT:** You fall in love with your solution and force it onto a problem
- **With DT:** You stay in love with the problem, and let solutions emerge

**Examples:**
- **Airbnb:** Started with empathy (travelers couldn't afford hotels) → defined the problem (affordable short-term stays) → prototyped (air mattresses)
- **Grab:** Started with empathy (taxi unavailability in KL) → defined (matching riders with available drivers)
- **Singapore example:** Doctor Anywhere started with empathy (long clinic wait times)

### Slide 7 — BDS Students' Advantage
- Design thinking is in your DNA
- This session will apply it to *business* problems — not just product design
- **Key shift:** Business model design is design thinking for how a company works

## Part 3: Stage 1 — Empathize (20 min)

### Slide 8 — What is Empathy in Entrepreneurship?
- NOT "I know what people want because I'm a person too"
- **Empathy = understanding someone else's experience deeply enough that you can feel their frustration**
- It's observational, not assumptive

### Slide 9 — Empathy Methods
| Method | What You Do | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Observation | Watch people in their natural environment | Uncovering hidden behaviors |
| Interviews | Ask open-ended questions (The Mom Test) | Understanding motivations |
| Immersion | Experience the problem yourself | Gaining personal insight |
| Shadowing | Follow a user through their day | Seeing the full experience |

**Pro Tip:** The best entrepreneurs are professional observers. When you're in line at a kopitiam, at MRT, or on campus — watch what frustrates people.

### Slide 10 — Empathy Exercise (10 min)
- **Scenario:** Design a better bus stop experience for SIT students
- **Walk through:** What would you observe?
  - How do students wait? (standing, sitting, pacing?)
  - What do they check? (phone, watch, bus app?)
  - What emotion do they show? (impatient, relaxed, stressed?)
  - What would you ask them? (NOT "would you like a better bus stop?")

## Part 4: Stage 2 — Define (15 min)

### Slide 11 — Define the Problem
- **Goal:** Write a problem statement that's specific enough to solve, broad enough to be creative
- **Tool:** "How Might We..." (HMW) statements
- **Structure:** [User] needs [need] because [insight]

### Slide 12 — HMW Statement Examples
| Bad HMW | Good HMW |
|---------|----------|
| "How might we build a better bus app?" | "How might we make waiting for the bus feel less wasted?" |
| "How might we sell cheaper food?" | "How might we help students eat well on a tight budget?" |
| "How might we create a study app?" | "How might we help students remember lecture content without cramming?" |

**Rule:** A good HMW doesn't suggest a solution. It describes a desired outcome.

### Slide 13 — Your Turn: HMW Practice (10 min)
- Take one frustration from the warm-up
- Turn it into 3 different HMW statements
- Share one with your neighbor — get feedback: "Does this suggest a solution, or keep the problem open?"

## Part 5: Stage 3 — Ideate (15 min)

### Slide 14 — Ideation = Quantity Over Quality
- **Brainstorming rules:**
  1. Defer judgment (no "that's stupid" — not even in your head)
  2. Encourage wild ideas (they lead to practical ones)
  3. Build on others' ideas ("yes, and...")
  4. Stay focused on the problem
  5. One conversation at a time

**Target:** 20+ ideas per problem statement

### Slide 15 — Ideation Techniques
- **Traditional brainstorm:** Say it, write it
- **Brainwriting:** Write ideas on paper, pass it around, others add to it
- **SCAMPER:** Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to another use, Eliminate, Reverse
- **Reverse brainstorming:** "How might we make this problem WORSE?"

### Slide 16 — Ideation Demo (5 min)
- Take one HMW: "How might we help students eat well on a tight budget?"
- Rapid-fire 10 ideas (instructor calls out, assistant writes on board):
  - Meal-sharing app
  - Campus community garden
  - Batch-cooking club
  - Keto meal delivery partnership
  - ...continues to 10+

## Part 6: Mini Design Sprint — Campus Problem (60 min)

### Slide 17 — Mini Design Sprint Instructions
- **Time:** 60 min (condensed)
- **Teams:** Groups of 3-4 (finalize team now!)
- **Format:** Walk through all 5 stages quickly

### Slide 18 — Stage 1: Empathize (10 min)
- Walk around campus (or your immediate area)
- Observe 3 frustrations or pain points
- Take notes — don't just talk, watch

### Slide 19 — Stage 2: Define (5 min)
- Pick ONE frustration
- Write 3 "How Might We..." statements
- Circle the best one

### Slide 20 — Stage 3: Ideate (10 min)
- Brainstorm 15+ solutions for your HMW
- No filtering yet

### Slide 21 — Stage 4: Prototype (15 min)
- Pick the most promising idea
- Sketch it on paper (3 min)
- Create a "fake" version to test:
  - Landing page (ad for the solution)
  - Paper mockup of the app/interface
  - Role-play script for the service
- Low fidelity is OK — actually, it's better

### Slide 22 — Stage 5: Test (10 min)
- Find a group that didn't see your idea
- Show them your prototype
- Ask: "What do you understand this to be?"
- Ask: "Would you use this? Why or why not?"
- Listen *without* defending your idea

### Slide 23 — Debrief (10 min)
- Each group: 1-minute share
- What did you learn? What surprised you?
- Would you pursue this idea further? Why?
