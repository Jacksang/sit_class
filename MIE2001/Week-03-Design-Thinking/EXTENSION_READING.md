# Week 3 — Extension Reading & Enrichment

## Core Reading (Required)

**"Design Thinking"** — Tim Brown (HBR, 2008)
- The article that popularized design thinking in business
- Covers the intersection of desirability, feasibility, and viability
- Available via SIT library database

## Enrichment Readings

### Design Thinking Theory
1. **"Change by Design"** — Tim Brown (book, Ch 1-3)
   - Full book-length treatment of design thinking
   - Rich case studies from IDEO's work
   - Ch 2 ("Getting Under the Skin") is excellent on empathy

2. **"Creative Confidence"** — Tom & David Kelley (book, Ch 1-4)
   - How to overcome "I'm not creative" mindset
   - Practical exercises for building creative muscle

3. **"The Design of Business"** — Roger Martin
   - How design thinking and analytical thinking complement each other
   - The "knowledge funnel": mystery → heuristic → algorithm

### Singapore Design Thinking Cases
4. **"How Grab Used Design Thinking to Build GrabCar"** — Think with Google
   - How Grab expanded beyond taxis by understanding rider needs
   - Prototyping and testing in Singapore market

5. **"Design Thinking at GovTech Singapore"** — GovTech blog
   - How Singapore government applies DT to public services
   - Case: Parking.sg, Moments of Life, Singpass redesign

6. **"Carousell's User Research: From Classifieds to Marketplace"** — Tech in Asia interview
   - How Carousell continuously observes user behavior

### Ideation Techniques
7. **"Where Good Ideas Come From"** — Steven Johnson (TED talk, 17 min)
   - The adjacent possible, slow hunches, and serendipity
   - Why coffee houses and collaboration spaces matter

8. **"Thinkertoys"** — Michael Michalko (book)
   - 30+ creative thinking techniques
   - Great reference for running ideation sessions

## Smart Brevity Version
If you only have 10 minutes:
1. Watch Tim Brown's HBR video summary (5 min)
2. Try the SCAMPER technique on ONE of your HMW statements (5 min)

## Design Thinking Tool Kit
| Stage | Recommended Tools |
|-------|-------------------|
| Empathize | Empathy map, User journey map, Observation log |
| Define | Point of View (POV) statement, How Might We (HMW) questions |
| Ideate | Brainstorming, Brainwriting, SCAMPER, Reverse brainstorming |
| Prototype | Paper prototype, Role-play, Landing page, Storyboard |
| Test | Think-aloud protocol, User feedback grid (Likes + Critiques + Questions + Ideas) |
