## Open Questions for Discussion

### Conceptual Questions
1. **Empathy ceiling:** Can you truly empathize with a user group that's very different from you? (e.g., an 18-year-old student designing for elderly users, or a Singaporean student designing for migrant workers)

2. **Design thinking in Asia:** Design thinking was developed in Silicon Valley. Does it transfer to Singapore? To China? To Indonesia? What cultural adaptations might be needed?

3. **Prototype fidelity:** When is a paper prototype *not enough*? When does fidelity actually matter for testing?

### Application Questions
4. **Your campus problem:** Of the frustrations you observed during the mini design sprint, which one is the most *commonly felt*? How many people would need to share that frustration to make it a viable business?

5. **Defining success:** If you were to test your prototype in 10 minutes instead of 60, would the outcome change? What's the minimum time for a valid test?

6. **Staying in love with the problem:** After completing the design sprint, are you more attached to your *problem* or to your *solution*? How do you know?

### Singapore Context Questions
7. SIT emphasizes applied learning and industry partnerships. How does this environment support (or hinder) the empathy stage of design thinking?

8. Many Singaporean startups solve Singapore-specific problems. Do you think there's a "minimum population size" for a problem to be worth solving? When should a Singapore startup think about regional/global markets?
