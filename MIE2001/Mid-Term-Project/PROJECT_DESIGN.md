# Mid-Term Project: Customer Discovery & Problem Validation

## Overview

**Weight:** Customer Discovery Report = 20% of final grade  
**Due:** Week 7 (submitted before mid-term pitch)  
**Format:** 3-4 pages, group submission  
**GOAL:** Demonstrate rigorous problem validation through customer interviews and market analysis

---

## Project Objective

By Week 7, each team must produce a Customer Discovery Report that validates (or invalidates) their chosen problem. This report forms the foundation for everything else in the semester — your BMC, your MVP, and your final pitch.

> **Key mindset:** A report that proves your initial idea wrong is MORE valuable than one that confirms it without evidence. Process > Outcome.

---

## Deliverable Requirements

### 1. Problem Statement (½ page)

**Answer: What problem are you solving, and for whom?**

- Clear, specific problem statement
- "How Might We..." statement (from Design Thinking)
- Evidence that this is a real problem (NOT just your opinion)
- **Good example:** "SIT first-year students who commute >1 hour struggle to find affordable, healthy lunch options near campus because existing options are expensive, limited, or require long waits."
- **Bad example:** "Students have trouble finding food." (too vague)

### 2. Customer Discovery (1-1½ pages)

**Answer: What did you learn from talking to potential customers?**

- Minimum **5 interviews** with target users
- Interview method described: how did you find them? What questions did you ask?
- Key quotes from interviews (anonymized)
- Patterns across interviews: what did multiple people say?
- **Surprising findings:** What contradicted your initial assumptions?
- **The Mom Test check:** Did you ask about their lives, not pitch your idea?

**Interview notes appendix (raw):** Include basic interview logs (date, person type, key points).

### 3. Market Sizing (½ page)

**Answer: Is this problem big enough to be a business?**

- **TAM:** Total addressable market (global)
- **SAM:** Serviceable available market (Singapore/region)
- **SOM:** Serviceable obtainable market (your realistic capture)
- Brief justification for each number

### 4. Competitive Analysis (½ page)

**Answer: Who else is solving this problem? How are you different?**

- Direct competitors (same solution)
- Indirect competitors (different solution to same problem)
- Status quo (what people do if they do nothing)
- Your differentiation: why would someone choose YOU?

### 5. Key Assumptions & Risks (¼ page)

**Answer: What's the riskiest assumption you still hold?**

- Top 3 assumptions you haven't fully tested
- What would disprove each one?
- What would you test next?

### 6. Appendix: Raw Interview Notes (as needed)

Interview logs with: date, person description (not name), key responses.

## Grading Rubric

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Satisfactory (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|---------------|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Problem clarity** | Specific, evidence-backed problem statement | Clear problem, some evidence | Vague problem, little evidence | No clear problem |
| **Interview quality** | 5+ target-user interviews, open-ended, rich quotes | 3-4 interviews, reasonably good | 1-2 interviews, leading questions | No interviews |
| **Learning articulation** | Shows how interviews changed understanding | Describes what was learned | Lists quotes without insight | No learning evident |
| **Market sizing** | TAM/SAM/SOM calculated, reasonable assumptions | Two tiers calculated | One tier calculated | No market sizing |
| **Competitive analysis** | Direct + indirect + status quo mapped | Two of three mapped | One type mapped | No analysis |
| **Structure & presentation** | Well-organized, compelling narrative | Clear structure | Some organization | Disorganized |

## Submission Checklist

Before submitting, confirm:
- [ ] Problem statement is specific, not generic
- [ ] At least 5 interviews conducted and documented
- [ ] Interview notes include dates and person type (anonymized)
- [ ] TAM/SAM/SOM calculated with explanations
- [ ] Competitive matrix completed
- [ ] Top 3 assumptions + risks identified
- [ ] Report is 3-4 pages (not counting appendix)
- [ ] All team members contributed (include contribution notes if helpful)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's a Problem |
|---------|-------------------|
| Interviewing only friends/classmates | Friends are biased — they want to help you |
| Asking "would you use this?" questions | People say yes to be nice (Mom Test violation) |
| Conducting 2 interviews instead of 5 | Not enough data for meaningful patterns |
| Using only secondary research (no interviews) | This report is about PRIMARY research |
| Presenting TAM without SOM | TAM is meaningless without realistic capture |
| Ignoring competitors | If you think you have no competitors, you haven't looked hard enough |
| Writing too much about your solution | This is a PROBLEM validation report, not a solution report |
