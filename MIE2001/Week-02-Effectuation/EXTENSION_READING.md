# Week 2 — Extension Reading & Enrichment

## Core Reading (Required)

**"What Makes Entrepreneurs Entrepreneurial?"** — Saras D. Sarasvathy (HBR)
- The foundational effectuation paper — should be read by now

## Enrichment Readings

### Effectuation Theory
1. **"Effectual Entrepreneurship"** — Read, Sarasvathy, Dew, Wiltbank & Ohlsson (book)
   - The definitive textbook on effectuation
   - Ch 1-3 cover the core framework with exercises
   - Available as SIT library e-book

2. **"Causation and Effectuation: Toward a Theoretical Shift from Economic Inevitability to Entrepreneurial Contingency"** — Sarasvathy (Academic, 2001)
   - The original academic paper (denser than the HBR version)
   - Good for students who want to go deeper on theory

3. **"The Affordable Loss Principle: Calculate the Downside, Not the Upside"** — Michael H. Morris (Entrepreneurship Theory & Practice)
   - Case studies showing affordable loss in action across cultures

### Applied Entrepreneurship Stories
4. **"The Lean Startup"** — Eric Ries (Ch 1-3)
   - How effectuation translates to modern startup methodology
   - Build-Measure-Learn as the practical expression of "Pilot-in-the-Plane"

5. **"Originals: How Non-Conformists Move the World"** — Adam Grant (Ch on risk)
   - Chapter on how entrepreneurs actually manage risk (spoiler: they're not fearless)
   - Data showing entrepreneurs who kept their day jobs were more likely to succeed

### Singapore Entrepreneur Stories
6. **"The Grab Story: How Anthony Tan Built a Super-App"** — Tech in Asia / DealStreetAsia
   - Full narrative of Grab's founding, pivots, and scaling
   - Good for case study assignments

7. **"They Did It In Singapore: How 20 Local Founders Built World-Class Companies"** — NUS Enterprise
   - Short biographies of Singapore founders
   - Each founder interview is 3-4 pages, easy to digest
