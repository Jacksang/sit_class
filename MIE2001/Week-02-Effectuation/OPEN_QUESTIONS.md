## Open Questions for Discussion

### Conceptual Questions
1. **Bird-in-Hand limitations:** Are there situations where starting with what you have is actually *worse* than planning for what you need? (e.g., medical devices, aerospace, regulated industries)

2. **Affordable Loss in Singapore context:** Does the higher cost of living in Singapore make the Affordable Loss principle harder to apply? If your rent is high, can you really afford to "lose" anything?

3. **Crazy Quilt ethics:** At what point does partnership cross into: (a) oversharing your idea, (b) giving away too much equity, (c) accidentally creating a competitor?

### Application Questions
4. **Cross cultural lens:** Effectuation was studied with US entrepreneurs. Do you think Singaporean entrepreneurs think differently? What about entrepreneurs in China, Indonesia, or India?

5. **The "pivot" principle:** Think of a business or product you know that completely changed direction (Slack, Instagram, Twitter). Which effectuation principle was at play in their pivot?

6. **Pilot-in-the-Plane + prediction:** Some industries DO require prediction (e.g., "how many masks will we need next month?"). How do entrepreneurs reconcile short-term control with the need to plan for scale?

### Singapore Context Questions
7. Singapore's government strongly supports entrepreneurship through grants (Startup SG Founder: \$50k matching). Does a government grant reduce the "affordable loss" or create dependencies on government funding?

8. Many successful Singapore startups were founded by immigrants or second-generation Singaporeans. Does "who you know" (network) create an unfair advantage in the Crazy Quilt principle?
